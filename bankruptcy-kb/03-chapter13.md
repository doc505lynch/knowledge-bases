# Chapter 13 Bankruptcy in California

## What Is Chapter 13?

Chapter 13 bankruptcy — the "reorganization" or "wage earner's" bankruptcy — allows individuals with regular income to repay all or part of their debts through a **court-approved repayment plan lasting 3 to 5 years**. Unlike Chapter 7, you do not surrender assets to a trustee. Instead, you keep your property and make monthly plan payments to a trustee who distributes funds to creditors.

At the end of the plan, remaining eligible unsecured debts are **discharged**.

## Who Is Eligible for Chapter 13?

### Income Requirement
You must have **regular income** — defined broadly to include wages, self-employment income, rental income, pension, Social Security, or any other regular source sufficient to fund a feasible repayment plan.

### Debt Limits (11 U.S.C. § 109(e))
As of 2024–2025 (amounts adjusted periodically):
- **Unsecured debts:** less than approximately **$465,275**
- **Secured debts:** less than approximately **$1,395,875**

### Prior Bankruptcy Restrictions
- Cannot have received a Chapter 13 discharge within the **past 2 years**
- Cannot have received a Chapter 7, 11, or 12 discharge within the **past 4 years**

## The Chapter 13 Repayment Plan

### Plan Duration
- **3 years** if current monthly income is below California's median for your household size
- **5 years** if income exceeds the median

### What the Plan Must Pay

| Debt Type | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Secured debts** (mortgage arrears, car loans) | Must be paid in full to retain the property |
| **Priority unsecured debts** | Must be paid 100% (taxes, domestic support) |
| **Non-priority unsecured debts** | At least what they would get in Chapter 7 liquidation |
| **Disposable income** | All income above allowed expenses goes to unsecured creditors |

## The Chapter 13 Process Step by Step

1. Credit counseling — complete before filing
2. File petition and plan — plan due within 14 days; $313 filing fee
3. Automatic stay activates immediately
4. Trustee assigned (standing trustee, not panel trustee)
5. 341 Meeting — typically 20–45 days after filing
6. Plan confirmation hearing — usually 45–60 days after 341 meeting
7. Plan payments begin — first payment due within 30 days of filing
8. Creditors file proofs of claim — within 70 days of 341 meeting
9. Complete the plan over 3–5 years
10. Complete debtor education course
11. File certification of plan completion
12. Discharge of remaining eligible debts

## Advantages of Chapter 13 Over Chapter 7

### Save Your Home
Chapter 13 is the **only way to cure a mortgage arrearage** through bankruptcy. The past-due amount is spread over 3–5 years while you resume current payments. Foreclosure halts immediately.

### Protect Non-Exempt Assets
If you have assets worth more than California's exemptions, you keep those assets by paying their non-exempt value through the plan.

### Lien Stripping
You may be able to **"strip off" a wholly unsecured second mortgage or HELOC** if the property value is equal to or less than the first mortgage balance. The junior lien is treated as unsecured and discharged at plan completion.

### Co-Debtor Stay
Chapter 13 provides a **co-debtor stay** that protects co-signers on consumer debts from collection during the case.

### Broader Discharge
Chapter 13 discharges certain debts that survive Chapter 7:
- Marital property settlement debts (non-support divorce obligations)
- Debts from willful and malicious injury to property (not persons)

## When Chapter 13 Is Better Than Chapter 7

| Situation | Choose Chapter 13 |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Behind on mortgage payments | Yes — catch up through the plan |
| Valuable non-exempt assets | Yes — protect by paying through the plan |
| Second mortgage that can be stripped | Yes — only available in Ch. 13 |
| Priority tax debts | Yes — pay over 5 years |
| Co-signer you want to protect | Yes — co-debtor stay applies |
| Previously filed Chapter 7 within 8 years | Yes — Ch. 13 available 4 years after Ch. 7 discharge |
