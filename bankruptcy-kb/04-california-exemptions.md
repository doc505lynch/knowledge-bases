# California Bankruptcy Exemptions

## Overview

California is one of the few states that has **opted out of the federal bankruptcy exemption system**. California filers must use one of **two state-specific exemption systems** and cannot mix and match between them.

Choosing the correct system is a critical decision that depends entirely on your specific assets.

## System 1 vs. System 2 — Quick Comparison

| Feature | System 1 (CCP § 703.140) | System 2 (CCP § 704) |
|---------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Best for** | Renters or those with little home equity | Homeowners with significant equity |
| **Homestead** | Up to $29,275 | $300,000–$600,000 |
| **Wildcard** | Up to ~$30,825 (unused homestead + $1,550) | $1,550 only |
| **Vehicle** | Up to $5,850 | Up to $3,625 |
| **Jewelry** | Up to $1,875 | Up to $8,725 (wedding rings) |

## System 1 — CCP § 703.140

### Homestead (System 1)
- Up to **$29,275** in real or personal property used as a residence
- Applies to mobile homes, boats, co-ops, or condos

### Motor Vehicle (System 1)
- Up to **$5,850** in one motor vehicle

### Household Goods and Furnishings (System 1)
- Up to **$700 per item** — furniture, appliances, clothing, books, animals, crops, musical instruments

### Jewelry (System 1)
- Up to **$1,875**

### Tools of the Trade (System 1)
- Up to **$2,925** in tools, implements, books, and equipment used in your trade

### Life Insurance (System 1)
- Up to **$14,875** in loan value of a life insurance contract

### Health Aids (System 1)
- **Unlimited** — professionally prescribed health aids

### Retirement Accounts (System 1)
- **100% exempt** for tax-exempt retirement accounts (IRAs, 401(k), pensions)
- IRAs and Roth IRAs federally capped at approximately **$1,512,350**

### Wildcard Exemption (System 1) — KEY ADVANTAGE
- **$1,550** in any property
- **Plus** any unused portion of the homestead exemption (up to $29,275)
- **Total potential wildcard: up to $30,825**
- Can be applied to cash, bank accounts, second vehicles, or any asset

## System 2 — CCP § 704

### Homestead Exemption (System 2) — KEY ADVANTAGE

As of January 1, 2021 (AB 1885):
- **Minimum:** $300,000
- **Maximum:** $600,000 (or the countywide median sale price for the prior calendar year, whichever is less)

**Important rules:**
- Applies only to the debtor's **principal residence**
- Debtor must have continuously lived in the home from petition date through the exemption hearing
- Does not protect against mortgage liens — only against trustee liquidation and judicial liens

### Motor Vehicle (System 2)
- Up to **$3,625** in one motor vehicle

### Household Furnishings (System 2)
- **Ordinary and necessary** household furnishings, apparel, and appliances — no specific dollar cap

### Jewelry and Heirlooms (System 2)
- **Wedding rings:** Up to $8,725
- **Other jewelry:** Up to $8,725 (combined with heirlooms and art)

### Tools of the Trade (System 2)
- Up to **$8,725** in tools, instruments, uniforms, books, and equipment of the trade

### Health Aids (System 2)
- **Unlimited** — prosthetics, health equipment

### Retirement Accounts (System 2)
- **100% exempt** — public and private retirement plans including:
  - CalPERS, CalSTRS
  - 401(k), 403(b), IRA, Roth IRA
  - SEP, SIMPLE, Keogh plans

### Wages (System 2)
- **75% of paid but uncollected wages** (or 40 times the California minimum hourly wage per week, whichever is greater)
- As of 2024: the greater of 75% of disposable earnings or approximately **$960/week** (based on $24/hr minimum wage × 40)

### Life Insurance (System 2)
- **100%** of benefits payable to a spouse, child, or dependent
- Cash surrender value exempt if policy has been in effect for at least **2 years** and premiums do not exceed **$500/month** on average

### Public Benefits (System 2)
- **100% exempt:** Social Security, unemployment, disability, workers' compensation, CalWORKS, CalFresh, SSI

### Personal Injury Claims (System 2)
- Up to **$29,275** for personal bodily injury claims

### Wildcard (System 2)
- **$1,550** only — far less than System 1's wildcard

## Dollar Amount Adjustment Schedule

All System 1 dollar amounts are adjusted every **3 years** on April 1. System 2 amounts are adjusted by the California Judicial Council. Always verify current amounts at **www.courts.ca.gov**.

---

## Community Property Considerations

California is a **community property state**:
- If only one spouse files, the automatic stay protects **all community property** from community creditors
- Both spouses' community property becomes property of the bankruptcy estate
- Separate property of the non-filing spouse is protected
- When both spouses file jointly, each spouse applies exemptions separately
