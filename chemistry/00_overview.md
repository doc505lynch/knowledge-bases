## What Is Chemistry?

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its properties, composition, structure, and the changes it undergoes during chemical reactions. It bridges physics (atomic/molecular behavior) and biology (life processes), and underpins materials science, medicine, environmental science, and engineering.

## Major Branches

| Branch | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **General/Physical Chemistry** | Fundamental laws: thermodynamics, kinetics, quantum mechanics applied to chemical systems |
| **Organic Chemistry** | Carbon-based compounds, functional groups, reaction mechanisms |
| **Inorganic Chemistry** | Non-carbon compounds, coordination chemistry, metals, minerals |
| **Analytical Chemistry** | Identification and quantification of substances; instrumentation |
| **Biochemistry** | Chemical processes in living organisms |
| **Nuclear Chemistry** | Radioactive decay, nuclear reactions, isotopes |
| **Computational Chemistry** | Modeling and simulation of molecular systems |
| **Environmental Chemistry** | Chemical processes in air, water, soil |
| **Polymer Chemistry** | Macromolecules, plastics, elastomers |
| **Electrochemistry** | Relationship between electricity and chemical reactions |

## States of Matter

- **Solid**: fixed shape and volume; particles in ordered lattice
- **Liquid**: fixed volume, variable shape; particles mobile but close
- **Gas**: variable shape and volume; particles far apart, random motion
- **Plasma**: ionized gas at extremely high temperature
- **Supercritical fluid**: above critical temperature and pressure; properties intermediate between liquid and gas

## Safety and Laboratory Fundamentals

- **GHS hazard symbols**: flammable, toxic, corrosive, oxidizer, environmental hazard, health hazard, explosive, compressed gas, irritant
- **PPE**: gloves, goggles, lab coat, fume hood for volatile substances
- **Disposal**: acids/bases neutralized before drain disposal; heavy metals as hazardous waste; organic solvents in dedicated waste containers
- **SDS (Safety Data Sheet)**: standardized document with hazard, handling, first-aid, disposal information for each chemical

## Dimensional Analysis and Significant Figures

**Dimensional analysis** (factor-label method): multiply by conversion factors so units cancel correctly.

**Significant figures rules**:
- All non-zero digits are significant
- Zeros between non-zero digits are significant
- Leading zeros are not significant
- Trailing zeros after decimal point are significant
- Exact numbers (counting, definitions) have infinite sig figs
