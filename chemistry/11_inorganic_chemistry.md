# Inorganic Chemistry

## Overview

Inorganic chemistry encompasses all compounds that are not primarily carbon-based. It includes:
- Main group element chemistry
- Transition metal chemistry and coordination compounds
- Organometallic chemistry
- Solid state/materials chemistry
- Bioinorganic chemistry

## Main Group Chemistry

### Hydrogen
- Three types of hydrides:
  - **Ionic (saline)**: formed with active metals (NaH, CaH₂); H⁻ is a strong base/reducing agent
  - **Covalent (molecular)**: HCl, H₂O, NH₃, SiH₄
  - **Metallic**: interstitial H in metal lattices (PdH₀.₆ used in H₂ storage)

### Group 1 (Alkali Metals)
- React with H₂O: 2M + 2H₂O → 2MOH + H₂ (reactivity: Li < Na < K < Rb < Cs)
- React with O₂: 
  - Li: 4Li + O₂ → 2Li₂O (oxide)
  - Na: 2Na + O₂ → Na₂O₂ (peroxide)
  - K, Rb, Cs: M + O₂ → MO₂ (superoxide)
- Form ionic halides, sulfates, carbonates

### Group 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)
- Harder, denser, higher MP than group 1
- Mg burns in CO₂ and N₂ (unlike most metals)
- Ca(OH)₂ (slaked lime): important for pH adjustment, concrete
- BaSO₄: insoluble, used as contrast agent in medical imaging

### Group 13
- **Boron**: metalloid; forms covalent compounds; electron deficient (Lewis acid)
  - BH₃ (borane), B₂H₆ (diborane): unusual 3-center 2-electron bonds
  - Boron halides: BF₃ (strong Lewis acid), BCl₃
  - Borax (Na₂B₄O₇): flux, cleaning agent
- **Aluminum**: forms amphoteric Al₂O₃ and Al(OH)₃; passivates in air
  - AlCl₃: strong Lewis acid, Friedel-Crafts catalyst
  - Aluminates: Al(OH)₄⁻ in basic solution

### Group 14
- **Carbon**: diamond, graphite, fullerenes, graphene, CNTs (see Atomic Structure)
- **Silicon**: semiconductor; SiO₂ (quartz, glass); silicates (minerals, cement)
  - Silicones: polymeric –[SiR₂–O]ₙ–; heat stable, hydrophobic
- **Germanium**: semiconductor; used in fiber optics
- **Tin**: +2 and +4 states; SnCl₂ (reducing agent)
- **Lead**: +2 preferred; PbO, PbSO₄; toxic heavy metal

### Group 15 (Pnictogens)
- **Nitrogen**: N₂ (inert); NH₃ (Haber process); HNO₃ (strong acid); NO, NO₂ (air pollutants)
  - Oxidation states: −3 to +5
- **Phosphorus**: white (P₄, reactive), red (stable polymer), black (layered)
  - H₃PO₄ (phosphoric acid), phosphates (fertilizers, DNA backbone)
- **Arsenic**: toxic metalloid; As₂O₃ (rat poison historically)

### Group 16 (Chalcogens)
- **Sulfur**: S₈ rings; H₂S (toxic gas); SO₂, SO₃ (acid rain); H₂SO₄ (contact process)
  - Oxidation states: −2 to +6
- **Selenium/Tellurium**: semiconductors; Se used in photovoltaics

### Group 17 (Halogens)
All form X₂ diatomics; electronegativity and oxidizing power decrease F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂

| Halogen | State at 25°C | Key compounds |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| F₂ | Gas | HF, NaF, CaF₂ (fluorite), UF₆ (nuclear) |
| Cl₂ | Gas | HCl, NaCl, NaOCl (bleach), PCBs |
| Br₂ | Liquid | HBr, NaBr, AgBr (photography) |
| I₂ | Solid | HI, NaI, thyroid hormones, antiseptic |

### Group 18 (Noble Gases)
- Generally inert; large atoms can form compounds (Xe)
- **XeF₂**: linear; synthesized 1962
- **XeF₄**: square planar
- **XeO₃**: trigonal pyramidal; explosive
- Kr/Xe used in lighting; He in cryogenics/balloons

## Transition Metal Chemistry

### Electron Configuration
d-block elements: filling 3d through 5d (and 4f, 5f for inner transition metals)

Key configurations:
- Cr: [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹ (not 3d⁴ 4s²; half-full d stability)
- Cu: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s¹ (not 3d⁹ 4s²; full d stability)

### Oxidation States
Transition metals exhibit multiple oxidation states. Common ones:
| Element | Common oxidation states |
|---------|------------------------|
| Ti | +2, +3, +4 |
| V | +2, +3, +4, +5 |
| Cr | +2, +3, +6 |
| Mn | +2, +3, +4, +6, +7 |
| Fe | +2, +3 |
| Co | +2, +3 |
| Ni | +2 |
| Cu | +1, +2 |
| Zn | +2 |

## Coordination Chemistry

### Coordination Compounds
Central metal ion + ligands (Lewis bases) coordinated by dative (coordinate) bonds.

**Ligand types**:
| Type | Denticity | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Monodentate | 1 | H₂O, NH₃, Cl⁻, CN⁻, CO |
| Bidentate | 2 | ethylenediamine (en), oxalate (ox), bipyridine (bipy) |
| Tridentate | 3 | diethylenetriamine (dien) |
| Tetradentate | 4 | triethylenetetramine (trien) |
| Hexadentate | 6 | EDTA⁴⁻ |
| Bridging | 2 metals | μ-Cl, μ-OH |

**Chelate effect**: polydentate ligands form more stable complexes than equivalent monodentate ligands (favorable ΔS due to ligand replacement releasing multiple solvent molecules)

### Nomenclature (IUPAC)
1. Cation before anion
2. Ligands in alphabetical order (by ligand name, not prefix)
3. Metal name with oxidation state in Roman numerals in parentheses
4. Anionic complexes end in -ate; neutral/cationic use metal name

**Prefixes**: di-, tri-, tetra-, penta-, hexa- for simple ligands; bis-, tris-, tetrakis- for complex ligands

**Examples**:
- [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺: hexaamminecobalt(III) ion
- [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻: hexacyanoferrate(II) ion (ferrocyanide)
- [PtCl₂(NH₃)₂]: diamminedichloridoplatinum(II) (cisplatin)
- K₂[PtCl₄]: potassium tetrachloridoplatinate(II)

### Common Geometries
| CN | Geometry | Example |
|----|----------|---------|
| 2 | Linear | [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺, [AuCl₂]⁻ |
| 4 | Tetrahedral | [FeCl₄]²⁻, [ZnCl₄]²⁻ |
| 4 | Square planar | [PtCl₄]²⁻, [Ni(CN)₄]²⁻ |
| 5 | Trigonal bipyramidal | [CuCl₅]³⁻ |
| 5 | Square pyramidal | [VO(acac)₂] |
| 6 | Octahedral | [Co(NH₃)₆]³⁺, [Fe(H₂O)₆]²⁺ |

### Crystal Field Theory (CFT)

Electrostatic model: ligands as point charges; d orbital energies split.

**Octahedral splitting**: ligands approach along ±x, ±y, ±z axes
- **t₂g** (dxy, dxz, dyz): lower energy (−0.4Δo each)
- **eg** (dx²-y², dz²): higher energy (+0.6Δo each)

**Δo** (crystal field splitting energy): depends on metal and ligands

**Spectrochemical series** (weak field → strong field, small Δo → large Δo):
I⁻ < Br⁻ < S²⁻ < SCN⁻ < Cl⁻ < F⁻ < OH⁻ < H₂O < NCS⁻ < NH₃ < en < bipy < phen < CN⁻ < CO

### High Spin vs. Low Spin (Octahedral d⁴–d⁷)
| dn | Strong field (large Δo) | Weak field (small Δo) |
|----|------------------------|----------------------|
| d⁴ | t₂g⁴ (low spin, 2 unpaired) | t₂g³eg¹ (high spin, 4 unpaired) |
| d⁵ | t₂g⁵ (low spin, 1 unpaired) | t₂g³eg² (high spin, 5 unpaired) |
| d⁶ | t₂g⁶ (low spin, 0 unpaired, diamagnetic) | t₂g⁴eg² (high spin, 4 unpaired) |
| d⁷ | t₂g⁶eg¹ (low spin, 1 unpaired) | t₂g⁵eg² (high spin, 3 unpaired) |

**Crystal Field Stabilization Energy (CFSE)**: energy gained by d electrons in split vs. spherical field

### Colors of Transition Metal Complexes
Complexes absorb visible light; complement of absorbed wavelength is observed color.
- [Ti(H₂O)₆]³⁺: absorbs green/yellow → appears purple
- [CrCl(NH₃)₅]²⁺: absorbs red → appears green
- Color depends on: metal, oxidation state, ligands (Δo magnitude)
- d⁰ and d¹⁰ complexes: colorless (no d-d transitions)

### Isomerism
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Geometric (cis/trans) | Different arrangement of ligands | cis/trans-[PtCl₂(NH₃)₂] |
| Facial/meridional | For MA₃B₃ octahedral | fac/mer-[Co(NH₃)₃Cl₃] |
| Optical (Δ/Λ) | Mirror image, non-superimposable | [Co(en)₃]³⁺ |
| Linkage | Same ligand bonded through different atoms | NO₂⁻ (N-bonded) vs ONO⁻ (O-bonded) |
| Ionization | Ions inside vs. outside coordination sphere | [Co(NH₃)₅Br]Cl₂ vs [Co(NH₃)₅Cl]BrCl |

## Organometallic Chemistry

### Key Concepts
- **Organometallic compounds**: direct metal-carbon bond
- **Effective Atomic Number (EAN) / 18-electron rule**: stable TM complexes tend to have 18 valence electrons
- **Hapticity (η)**: number of contiguous atoms coordinated to metal
  - η¹-allyl, η³-allyl, η⁵-cyclopentadienyl (Cp), η⁶-benzene

### Electron Counting
Ionic model: M⁺ˣ with formal ligand charges counted  
Covalent model: neutral metal, neutral ligand e⁻ count  

**Common ligands (covalent model)**:
| Ligand | e⁻ donated |
|--------|-----------|
| CO, PR₃, NR₃ | 2 |
| Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻ | 2 (as X-type, 1 when neutral) |
| η²-alkene | 2 |
| η³-allyl | 3 (+ 1 for anionic form) |
| η⁵-Cp | 5 |
| η⁶-arene | 6 |

### Famous Organometallics
- **Ferrocene** [Fe(η⁵-Cp)₂]: 18e⁻, sandwich compound; redox active
- **Metal carbonyls**: M(CO)ₙ; 18e⁻; Ni(CO)₄, Fe(CO)₅, Cr(CO)₆
- **Wilkinson's catalyst**: [RhCl(PPh₃)₃]; homogeneous hydrogenation
- **Grubbs catalyst**: Ru carbene complex; olefin metathesis
- **Zeise's salt**: [PtCl₃(η²-C₂H₄)]⁻; first isolated olefin complex (1827)

| Perovskite (CaTiO₃) | Complex | BaTiO₃ (ferroelectric), high-Tc superconductors |

### Ionic Radii and Pauling Rules
1. Bond length ≈ r_cation + r_anion
2. Coordination number determined by radius ratio (r+/r−)
3. Electrostatic valence rule: sum of bond strengths at each anion = anion charge
4. Sharing of edges/faces between polyhedra destabilizes structure
5. Different cation types minimize contact (avoid sharing features)

### Defects in Crystals
- **Schottky defect**: equal number of cation + anion vacancies
- **Frenkel defect**: ion moves to interstitial site (creates vacancy + interstitial)
- **Non-stoichiometric**: e.g., FeO → Fe₀.₉₅O (iron vacancies; mix of Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺)
