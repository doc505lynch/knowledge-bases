# The Periodic Table

## Organization

The periodic table arranges 118 known elements by increasing atomic number (Z) in rows (periods) and columns (groups) that reflect recurring (periodic) properties.

- **Periods (rows)**: 1–7; period number = highest principal quantum number (n) for valence electrons
- **Groups (columns)**: 1–18; elements in same group have same valence electron configuration and similar chemical behavior

## Block Structure

| Block | Groups | Subshell being filled |
|-------|--------|-----------------------|
| s-block | 1–2 (+ He) | s orbital |
| p-block | 13–18 | p orbitals |
| d-block | 3–12 | d orbitals (transition metals) |
| f-block | Lanthanides/Actinides | f orbitals |

## Group Names and Properties

| Group | Name | Valence e⁻ | Key Property |
|-------|------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | Alkali metals | 1 (ns¹) | Highly reactive metals; form M⁺ ions; react violently with water |
| 2 | Alkaline earth metals | 2 (ns²) | Reactive metals; form M²⁺ ions |
| 3–12 | Transition metals | Variable | d electrons; multiple oxidation states; colored compounds; catalytic |
| 13 | Boron group | 3 | Mix of metal/nonmetal character |
| 14 | Carbon group | 4 | From nonmetal (C) to metal (Pb) |
| 15 | Nitrogen/Pnictogens | 5 | From nonmetal (N) to metalloid/metal |
| 16 | Chalcogens | 6 | O is most electronegative; S/Se/Te form many oxyanions |
| 17 | Halogens | 7 (ns²np⁵) | Very reactive nonmetals; form X⁻ ions; diatomic molecules |
| 18 | Noble gases | 8 (ns²np⁶) | Chemically inert (full valence shell) |

## Periodic Trends

### Atomic Radius
- **Across period (→)**: decreases (increasing Zeff pulls electrons closer)
- **Down group (↓)**: increases (new electron shell added)
- Largest: Cs, Fr (bottom-left); Smallest: He, F (top-right)

### Ionic Radius
- Cations smaller than parent atom (lose electrons, less repulsion)
- Anions larger than parent atom (gain electrons, more repulsion)
- Isoelectronic series (same e⁻ count): radius decreases as Z increases

### Ionization Energy (IE)
Energy required to remove an electron from a gaseous atom:
X(g) → X⁺(g) + e⁻

- **Across period (→)**: generally increases (higher Zeff)
- **Down group (↓)**: decreases (outer electrons farther from nucleus)
- **Exceptions**: Group 13 < Group 2 (removing from p vs. s); Group 16 < Group 15 (pairing repulsion)

**Successive IEs**: large jump when removing core electron (indicates number of valence electrons)

### Electron Affinity (EA)
Energy change when electron added to neutral gaseous atom:
X(g) + e⁻ → X⁻(g)

- More negative EA = greater tendency to gain electron
- Halogens have most negative EA (especially Cl)
- Noble gases have positive EA (unfavorable)
- Trend: generally increases across period, decreases down group (less regular than IE)

### Electronegativity (EN)
Ability of atom in a bond to attract shared electrons.

**Pauling scale**: F = 4.0 (highest); Cs/Fr ≈ 0.7 (lowest)
- Increases across period, decreases down group
- EN difference > 1.7 → ionic bond; 0.4–1.7 → polar covalent; < 0.4 → nonpolar covalent

### Metallic Character
- Increases down group, decreases across period
- Metals (left/bottom): lustrous, malleable, ductile, good conductors, form cations
- Nonmetals (right/top): brittle (if solid), poor conductors, form anions or covalent bonds
- Metalloids (staircase boundary): Si, Ge, As, Sb, Te, Po, At — semiconductor behavior

| Category | Examples | Properties |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Alkali metals | Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs | Soft, low density, react with H₂O |
| Alkaline earth | Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba | Harder, higher MP than alkali |
| Transition metals | Fe, Cu, Zn, Cr, Ni | Variable oxidation states, colored ions |

| Noble gases | He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn | Monatomic, inert |

## Key Elements and Their Chemistry

### Hydrogen (H, Z=1)
- Unique: placed in group 1 (loses e⁻) but also resembles group 17 (gains e⁻)
- Forms H₂ (diatomic); HX acids with halogens; H₂O with oxygen
- Oxidation states: +1 (in acids, most compounds), −1 (metal hydrides), 0 (H₂)

### Carbon (C, Z=6)
- Forms 4 bonds; basis of organic chemistry
- Allotropes: diamond (sp³, tetrahedral), graphite (sp², planar sheets), fullerenes, graphene, CNTs
- Isotopes: ¹²C (98.9%), ¹³C (1.1%), ¹⁴C (radioactive, used in dating)

### Nitrogen (N, Z=7)
- Diatomic N₂ (triple bond, very stable); 78% of atmosphere
- Oxidation states: −3 (NH₃) to +5 (NO₃⁻)
- Essential for amino acids, nucleotides

### Oxygen (O, Z=8)
- Second most electronegative; diatomic O₂; ozone O₃
- Oxidation states: −2 (most compounds), −1 (peroxides), 0 (O₂)

### Iron (Fe, Z=26)
- Most common transition metal in biology and industry
- Oxidation states: +2 (Fe²⁺, ferrous), +3 (Fe³⁺, ferric)
- Forms rust (Fe₂O₃·nH₂O) via oxidation

### Sodium (Na, Z=11)
- Soft alkali metal; reacts violently with water: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂
- NaCl (table salt), NaOH (lye), Na₂CO₃ (soda ash)

## Periodic Table Sections Worth Memorizing

**Diatomic elements** (HOFBrINCl or BrINClHOF): H₂, O₂, F₂, Br₂, I₂, N₂, Cl₂

**Polyatomic elements**: P₄ (white phosphorus), S₈ (rhombic sulfur)

**Radioactive elements**: Tc (Z=43), Pm (Z=61), all elements Z≥84

**Synthetic elements**: all Z > 94 (post-plutonium) are fully synthetic
