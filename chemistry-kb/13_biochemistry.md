# Biochemistry

## Overview

Biochemistry studies the chemical processes occurring within and related to living organisms. The four major classes of biomolecules are:
- **Carbohydrates** (sugars, polysaccharides)
- **Lipids** (fats, phospholipids, steroids)
- **Proteins** (amino acids, enzymes)
- **Nucleic acids** (DNA, RNA)

## Water and pH

Water is essential for life due to:
- High specific heat (temperature buffer)
- High heat of vaporization (cooling)
- Excellent solvent (hydrophilic solutes)
- Polar nature enabling H-bonding
- Amphoteric character (Kw = 10⁻¹⁴)

**Biological pH buffers**:
- Blood: 7.35–7.45 (HCO₃⁻/H₂CO₃ system)
- Intracellular: ~7.2 (phosphate buffer)
- Lysosomes: ~5.0
- Stomach: ~2.0

## Amino Acids and Proteins

### Amino Acids
All have: α-amino group (–NH₃⁺), α-carboxyl group (–COO⁻), α-hydrogen, and R group (side chain)

**General structure**: H₂N–CH(R)–COOH (L-configuration predominant in nature)

**20 standard amino acids by R group**:

| Category | Amino acids |
|----------|------------|
| Nonpolar, aliphatic | Gly, Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Pro, Met |
| Aromatic | Phe, Tyr, Trp |
| Polar, uncharged | Ser, Thr, Cys, Asn, Gln |
| Positively charged | Lys, Arg, His |
| Negatively charged | Asp, Glu |

### Peptide Bond
Condensation between α-COOH and α-NH₂; releases H₂O; C–N bond has partial double bond character (planar, no rotation).
- N-terminus: free amino group (written left)
- C-terminus: free carboxyl group (written right)

### Protein Structure Levels

**Primary**: amino acid sequence (determined by DNA)

**Secondary**: local folding patterns:
- **α-helix**: right-handed helix; 3.6 aa/turn; H-bond between i and i+4 C=O and N–H
- **β-sheet**: parallel or antiparallel strands; H-bonds between strands
- **β-turn**: 4-residue loop reversing chain direction; often Pro and Gly

**Tertiary**: overall 3D fold of single polypeptide; stabilized by:
- Hydrophobic interactions (buried nonpolar side chains)
- Disulfide bonds (Cys–Cys)
- H-bonds
- Ionic interactions (salt bridges)

**Quaternary**: association of multiple polypeptide subunits (e.g., hemoglobin: 2α + 2β subunits)

### Protein Folding and Denaturation
- **Denaturation**: unfolding without breaking peptide bonds; caused by heat, pH extremes, detergents, denaturants (urea, guanidinium chloride)
- **Chaperones**: proteins that assist proper folding (Hsp70, GroEL)
- **Prions**: misfolded proteins that propagate misfolding in others

### Amino Acid pKa and Isoelectric Point
Each ionizable group has pKa:
```
pI (isoelectric point) = average of pKas flanking neutral form
```
- At pH = pI: no net charge; minimum solubility
- pH < pI: net positive charge → migrates toward cathode in electrophoresis
- pH > pI: net negative charge → migrates toward anode

## Enzymes

### Characteristics
- Biological catalysts (almost always proteins; ribozymes are RNA)
- Highly specific (substrate, reaction type, stereochemistry)
- Reduce activation energy; do not change equilibrium
- Organized into active site and allosteric sites

### Michaelis-Menten Kinetics
E + S ⇌ ES → E + P

```
v = Vmax[S] / (Km + [S])
```

- **Vmax**: maximum rate (at saturating [S]); Vmax = kcat[E]total
- **Km**: Michaelis constant = [S] at half-Vmax; approximates KD for ES complex
- **kcat** (turnover number): reactions per enzyme per second
- **kcat/Km**: catalytic efficiency; upper limit ~10⁸–10⁹ M⁻¹s⁻¹ (diffusion limit)

**Lineweaver-Burk plot**: 1/v vs 1/[S] → slope = Km/Vmax, x-int = −1/Km, y-int = 1/Vmax

### Enzyme Inhibition
| Type | Effect on Km | Effect on Vmax | Reversal |
|------|-------------|----------------|---------|
| Competitive | Increase (apparent) | No change | Yes (excess substrate) |
| Uncompetitive | Decrease | Decrease | — |
| Noncompetitive | No change | Decrease | — |
| Mixed | Change | Decrease | — |
| Irreversible | — | Decreases permanently | No |

**Competitive**: inhibitor binds active site (looks like substrate)
**Noncompetitive**: inhibitor binds allosteric site; prevents catalysis but not substrate binding

### Enzyme Cofactors
| Type | Example | Role |
|------|---------|------|
| Metal ions | Zn²⁺, Fe²⁺, Mg²⁺ | Lewis acid catalysis, electron transfer |
| Coenzymes (loosely bound) | NAD⁺, FAD, CoA, ATP | Electron/group carriers |
| Prosthetic groups (tightly bound) | Heme, biotin, pyridoxal phosphate | Integral part of enzyme |
| Vitamins (precursors) | B vitamins, ascorbate | Precursors to coenzymes |

### Enzyme Regulation
- **Allosteric regulation**: effector binds remote site; changes shape (conformational change)
  - Allosteric enzymes often have sigmoidal kinetics (Hill equation, cooperativity)
- **Covalent modification**: phosphorylation (kinase/phosphatase), acetylation, glycosylation
- **Proteolytic activation**: zymogens (proenzymes) → active enzyme (e.g., pepsinogen → pepsin)
- **Feedback inhibition**: end product inhibits first committed step (often allosteric)

## Carbohydrates

### Classification
- **Monosaccharides**: single sugar units; general formula (CH₂O)ₙ
  - **Aldoses**: aldehyde group (glucose, galactose, ribose)
  - **Ketoses**: ketone group (fructose, ribulose)
  - **D/L designation**: configuration at chiral center farthest from carbonyl
- **Disaccharides**: two monosaccharides joined by glycosidic bond
- **Oligosaccharides**: 3–10 units
- **Polysaccharides**: >10 units (starch, glycogen, cellulose, chitin)

### Important Monosaccharides
| Sugar | Type | Key role |
|-------|------|----------|
| D-Glucose | Aldohexose | Primary cellular energy source |
| D-Fructose | Ketohexose | Fruit sugar; in sucrose |
| D-Galactose | Aldohexose | In lactose; brain lipids |
| D-Ribose | Aldopentose | RNA backbone |
| 2-Deoxyribose | Aldopentose | DNA backbone |
| N-Acetylglucosamine | Amino sugar | Cell walls, chitin |

### Glycosidic Bonds and Polysaccharides
- **α-1,4 glycosidic bond**: starch (amylose/amylopectin), glycogen (+ α-1,6 branches)
- **β-1,4 glycosidic bond**: cellulose (humans cannot digest — no β-glucosidase)
- **Glycogen**: branched α-1,4 + α-1,6; liver and muscle energy storage
- **Chitin**: β-1,4-N-acetylglucosamine; insect exoskeleton, fungal cell walls

### Blood Group Antigens
ABO blood groups: oligosaccharide antigens on red blood cell surface
- A: GalNAc added to H antigen
- B: Gal added to H antigen
- O: only H antigen
- AB: both GalNAc and Gal

## Lipids

### Fatty Acids
Long-chain carboxylic acids (12–24 carbons); even number due to biosynthesis from acetyl units

| Type | Example | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| Saturated | Palmitic (C16:0), Stearic (C18:0) | Solid at room T; straight chains pack tightly |
| Monounsaturated | Oleic (C18:1, Δ9, cis) | Liquid; Mediterranean diet |
| Polyunsaturated (PUFA) | Linoleic (C18:2, ω-6), α-Linolenic (C18:3, ω-3) | Essential fatty acids |
| Trans fat | Elaidic (C18:1, Δ9, trans) | Industrially hydrogenated; cardiovascular risk |
| EPA, DHA | ω-3 PUFAs | Fish oil; anti-inflammatory; brain |

### Lipid Classes

**Triacylglycerols (triglycerides)**: 3 fatty acids + glycerol; energy storage  
**Phospholipids**: 2 fatty acids + glycerol + phosphate + head group; membrane bilayer  
- Phosphatidylcholine (lecithin): most abundant membrane phospholipid  
- Phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylserine, phosphatidylinositol  

**Sphingolipids**: sphingosine backbone; in myelin (sphingomyelin), cell signaling (ceramide, S1P)  
**Cholesterol**: sterol; membrane fluidity regulator; precursor to steroid hormones, bile acids, vitamin D  
**Eicosanoids**: C20 lipids from arachidonic acid; prostaglandins, thromboxanes, leukotrienes (inflammatory mediators)

### Membranes
- **Fluid mosaic model**: phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins
- **Fluidity**: increased by: shorter chains, unsaturation (especially cis double bonds), less cholesterol
- **Membrane proteins**: integral (transmembrane), peripheral, lipid-anchored
- **Lipid rafts**: cholesterol + sphingolipid-enriched microdomains; signaling platforms

## Nucleic Acids

### Nucleotides
Nucleotide = sugar (ribose or deoxyribose) + phosphate + nitrogenous base

**Purines** (double ring): Adenine (A), Guanine (G)  
**Pyrimidines** (single ring): Cytosine (C), Thymine (T, DNA only), Uracil (U, RNA only)

**Base pairing** (Watson-Crick):
- A–T (or A–U): 2 hydrogen bonds
- G–C: 3 hydrogen bonds (stronger; higher GC content → higher Tm)

### DNA Structure
- B-form double helix (Watson-Crick, 1953): right-handed; 10 bp/turn; 3.4 nm/turn
- Major and minor grooves: protein binding sites
- Antiparallel strands: one 5'→3', other 3'→5'
- **Tm** (melting temperature): temperature at which 50% of dsDNA is single-stranded; increases with %GC and length
- **A-form**: right-handed, compact (RNA duplex, DNA-RNA hybrids)
- **Z-form**: left-handed; alternating purine-pyrimidine; biological significance unclear

### RNA Types
| Type | Function |
|------|----------|
| mRNA (messenger) | Codes for protein (translated by ribosome) |
| tRNA (transfer) | Carries amino acids; anticodon loop |
| rRNA (ribosomal) | Structural + catalytic component of ribosome |
| snRNA | Splicing (spliceosome) |
| miRNA/siRNA | Post-transcriptional gene regulation |
| lncRNA | Gene regulation (chromatin remodeling, etc.) |
| Ribozymes | Catalytic RNA (e.g., RNase P, group I introns) |

## Metabolism

### Glycolysis (Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas Pathway)
Location: cytoplasm  
Input: 1 glucose → Output: 2 pyruvate + 2 ATP (net) + 2 NADH

Key enzymes:
- Hexokinase (step 1): glucose → glucose-6-phosphate; regulated by product
- Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1, step 3): rate-limiting; inhibited by ATP/citrate; activated by AMP/fructose-2,6-bisphosphate
- Pyruvate kinase (step 10): PEP → pyruvate + ATP

Pyruvate fate:
- Aerobic: pyruvate → acetyl-CoA (PDH complex) → TCA cycle
- Anaerobic: pyruvate → lactate (animals) or ethanol + CO₂ (yeast)

### TCA Cycle (Krebs/Citric Acid Cycle)
Location: mitochondrial matrix  
Per acetyl-CoA: 3 NADH + 1 FADH₂ + 1 GTP + 2 CO₂

Key steps: citrate synthase, isocitrate dehydrogenase, α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase (all regulated by NADH, ATP, succinyl-CoA)

### Oxidative Phosphorylation
Location: inner mitochondrial membrane

**Electron transport chain (ETC)**:
NADH → Complex I → CoQ → Complex III → Cyt c → Complex IV → O₂
FADH₂ → Complex II → CoQ

H⁺ pumped to intermembrane space → proton gradient (chemiosmotic potential)
ATP synthase (Complex V): H⁺ flow down gradient → ATP synthesis

**ATP yield** (theoretical per glucose, aerobic): ~30–32 ATP
NADH: ~2.5 ATP; FADH₂: ~1.5 ATP

### β-Oxidation (Fatty Acid Oxidation)
Location: mitochondrial matrix  
Palmitate (C16): 7 cycles → 8 acetyl-CoA + 7 NADH + 7 FADH₂  
Net: ~106 ATP per palmitate

### Fatty Acid Synthesis
Location: cytoplasm (fatty acid synthase, FAS)  
Malonyl-CoA building block; NADPH as reductant  
Key regulation: acetyl-CoA carboxylase (carboxylates acetyl-CoA → malonyl-CoA); activated by citrate, inhibited by fatty acyl-CoA

### Nitrogen Metabolism
**Amino acid catabolism**: transamination → α-ketoacids (TCA entry) + glutamate  
**Urea cycle** (liver): converts toxic NH₃ → urea (excreted in urine)  
Aspartate + citrulline → argininosuccinate → arginine + fumarate → arginine → urea + ornithine

### Photosynthesis
**Light reactions** (thylakoid membrane): H₂O → O₂ + NADPH + ATP  
**Calvin cycle** (stroma): CO₂ + NADPH + ATP → G3P → glucose  
- RuBisCO: key enzyme for CO₂ fixation (3-PGA from CO₂ + RuBP)
- 3 CO₂ → 1 G3P (net); 6 turns → 1 fructose-6-phosphate

## DNA Replication, Transcription, Translation

### Replication (DNA → DNA)
- **Semi-conservative**: each new DNA has one old + one new strand
- **Key enzymes**: helicase (unwinds), primase (RNA primer), DNA polymerase III (extends 5'→3'), ligase (joins Okazaki fragments)
- **Leading strand**: continuous synthesis; **lagging strand**: Okazaki fragments
- Fidelity: ~1 error per 10⁹ bp (with proofreading)

### Transcription (DNA → RNA)
- Template strand (antisense) read 3'→5'; RNA synthesized 5'→3'
- RNA polymerase (bacteria: single enzyme; eukaryotes: Pol I, II, III for rRNA, mRNA, tRNA respectively)
- **Promoter** elements (TATA box, −35 element in bacteria) recognized by sigma factor/transcription factors
- **Eukaryotic mRNA processing**: 5' cap, 3' poly-A tail, splicing of introns

### Translation (mRNA → Protein)
- **Ribosome**: large subunit (peptidyl transferase) + small subunit (mRNA decoding)
- **Codons**: 3 nucleotides → 1 amino acid; 64 codons; 61 sense + 3 stop (UAA, UAG, UGA)
- **Start codon**: AUG (methionine)
- **tRNA**: anticodon complements codon; aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase charges tRNA with amino acid
- **Wobble hypothesis**: third codon position allows some mismatching

### Genetic Code Features
- Degenerate: multiple codons for same amino acid
- Non-overlapping, read in frame
- Universal (nearly): same across all life

## Signal Transduction

### Second Messengers
| Messenger | Source | Effects |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| cAMP | Adenylyl cyclase from ATP | Activates PKA; activates/inhibits various processes |
| cGMP | Guanylyl cyclase from GTP | Smooth muscle relaxation (NO pathway), vision |
| IP₃ | PLC cleaves PIP₂ | Releases Ca²⁺ from ER |
| DAG | PLC cleaves PIP₂ | Activates PKC |
| Ca²⁺ | Released from ER or extracellular | Calmodulin activation; muscle contraction |
| PIP₃ | PI3K | Activates Akt (PKB); survival signaling |

### Major Signaling Pathways
- **GPCR/cAMP**: epinephrine/β-adrenergic → Gs → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA
- **RTK/MAPK**: growth factors → receptor tyrosine kinase → Ras → Raf → MEK → ERK
- **PI3K/Akt**: insulin, growth factors → PI3K → PIP₃ → PDK1 → Akt → mTOR (growth/survival)
- **Wnt**: developmental pathway; β-catenin stabilization → transcription
- **NF-κB**: inflammatory pathway; IκB degradation → NF-κB nuclear entry
- **JAK-STAT**: cytokines → receptor → JAK → STAT dimerization → transcription
