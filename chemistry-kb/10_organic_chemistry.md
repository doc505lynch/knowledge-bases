# Organic Chemistry

## Introduction

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds. Carbon is uniquely suited to forming the backbone of life and materials because it:
- Forms 4 stable covalent bonds
- Bonds to itself in chains, rings, and networks
- Bonds to H, O, N, S, halogens, and metals
- Forms single, double, and triple bonds

## IUPAC Nomenclature

### Alkanes
**Prefix** based on carbon count: meth-1, eth-2, prop-3, but-4, pent-5, hex-6, hept-7, oct-8, non-9, dec-10

**Steps**:
1. Find longest continuous carbon chain (parent chain)
2. Number from end closest to first branch
3. Name substituents as prefixes (methyl-, ethyl-, propyl-, etc.)
4. List substituents alphabetically with locant numbers
5. Use di-, tri-, tetra- for multiples of same substituent

**Example**: CH₃CH(CH₃)CH₂CH₃ = 2-methylbutane

### Cycloalkanes
Prefix "cyclo-": cyclopropane (3C), cyclobutane, cyclopentane, cyclohexane

### Alkenes and Alkynes
- Double bond: -ene; triple bond: -yne
- Number chain from end closer to multiple bond
- E/Z (or cis/trans) for alkene stereochemistry

### Stereochemistry
**E (entgegen)**: higher priority groups on opposite sides of double bond  
**Z (zusammen)**: higher priority groups on same side  
**Priority**: Cahn-Ingold-Prelog (CIP) rules — higher atomic number gets higher priority

### Chirality (Handedness)
- **Chiral center**: sp³ carbon with 4 different substituents
- **Enantiomers**: non-superimposable mirror images; same physical properties except optical rotation
- **R/S designation**: assign priorities by CIP rules; R (rectus) = clockwise; S (sinister) = counterclockwise
- **Optical activity**: [α] = angle of plane-polarized light rotation; (+) dextrorotatory, (−) levorotatory
- **Racemic mixture**: 50/50 enantiomers; no optical activity

## Alkanes and Cycloalkanes

- General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ (alkanes), CₙH₂ₙ (cycloalkanes)
- **Conformations** (rotation around C–C bonds):
  - **Staggered**: Newman projection — lower energy (gauche > anti for propane)
  - **Eclipsed**: higher energy (torsional strain)
- **Cyclohexane chair conformations**: axial and equatorial positions; chair flipping exchanges them
  - Large substituents prefer equatorial position (less steric strain)
  - Disubstituted: trans-1,4-diaxial has most ring strain; trans diequatorial preferred

### Reactions of Alkanes
- **Combustion**: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (highly exothermic)
- **Halogenation** (radical mechanism):
  1. Initiation: X₂ → 2X· (UV or heat)
  2. Propagation: X· + RH → HX + R·; R· + X₂ → RX + X·
  3. Termination: radicals combine

## Alkenes and Alkynes

- Alkenes: general CₙH₂ₙ; planar sp² carbons; restricted rotation
- Alkynes: general CₙH₂ₙ₋₂; linear sp carbons

### Electrophilic Addition Reactions of Alkenes
Alkenes are nucleophiles (π electrons attack electrophiles)

| Reaction | Reagent | Product | Regioselectivity |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| Hydrohalogenation | HX (HCl, HBr, HI) | Alkyl halide | Markovnikov |
| Hydration | H₂O, H⁺ | Alcohol | Markovnikov |
| Halogenation | Cl₂, Br₂ | Dihalide (vicinal) | Anti addition |
| Halohydrin formation | X₂, H₂O | Halohydrin | Markovnikov, anti |
| Hydrogenation | H₂, Pd/C or Pt | Alkane | Syn addition |
| Epoxidation | MCPBA (peroxyacid) | Epoxide | Syn |
| Dihydroxylation | OsO₄, then H₂O₂ | 1,2-diol | Syn |
| Anti-dihydroxylation | 1) MCPBA, 2) H₃O⁺ | 1,2-diol (anti) | Anti |
| Ozonolysis | O₃, then Zn/H₂O or Me₂S | Aldehydes/ketones | Cleaves double bond |

**Markovnikov's Rule**: H adds to the C with more H atoms (carbocation on more substituted C)
**Zaitsev's Rule**: major elimination product has most substituted double bond

### Carbocation Stability
Tertiary > Secondary > Primary > Methyl (more alkyl groups stabilize by hyperconjugation/induction)

### Alkynes
- Terminal alkynes: weakly acidic (pKa ≈ 25); react with NaNH₂ to form acetylide anion
- Hydration (H₂SO₄, HgSO₄, H₂O): gives ketone (Markovnikov) or aldehyde (terminal only with hydroboration/oxidation)
- Can be reduced to cis-alkene (Lindlar's catalyst: H₂/Pd-CaCO₃/quinoline) or trans-alkene (Na in NH₃)

## Aromatic Compounds

### Aromaticity (Hückel's Rule)
Aromatic if: cyclic, fully conjugated, planar, and has 4n + 2 π electrons (n = 0, 1, 2, …)
- Benzene: 6 π electrons (n=1) ✓
- Naphthalene: 10 π electrons (n=2) ✓
- Cyclobutadiene: 4 π electrons (antiaromatic) ✗
- Cyclopentadienyl anion (C₅H₅⁻): 6 π electrons ✓

### Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution (EAS)
ArH + E⁺ → ArE + H⁺

| Reaction | Reagents | Product |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Halogenation | Cl₂ or Br₂ + FeCl₃/FeBr₃ | ArCl, ArBr |
| Nitration | HNO₃ + H₂SO₄ | ArNO₂ |
| Sulfonation | H₂SO₄ (fuming) or SO₃ | ArSO₃H |
| Friedel-Crafts alkylation | RCl + AlCl₃ | ArR |
| Friedel-Crafts acylation | RCOCl + AlCl₃ | ArCOR |

### Directing Effects (Substituent Effects)
- **Ortho/para directors** (activate ring): –OH, –OR, –NH₂, –NHR, –NR₂, –alkyl
- **Ortho/para directors** (deactivate ring, halogen): –F, –Cl, –Br, –I (donate by resonance, withdraw by induction)
- **Meta directors** (deactivate ring): –NO₂, –CN, –COOH, –SO₃H, –CHO, –COR, –CF₃

## Alcohols

Primary (RCH₂OH), Secondary (R₂CHOH), Tertiary (R₃COH)

### Reactions of Alcohols

| Reaction | Reagent | Product |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Oxidation (1°) | PCC, or KMnO₄ | Aldehyde (PCC) or carboxylic acid (KMnO₄) |
| Oxidation (2°) | KMnO₄, K₂Cr₂O₇, or PCC | Ketone |
| Oxidation (3°) | — | No reaction |
| Dehydration | H₃PO₄/heat or H₂SO₄ | Alkene (Zaitsev) |
| Substitution | HX, PBr₃, SOCl₂ | Alkyl halide |
| Ether formation | R–OH + H₂SO₄ | R–O–R (Williamson) |
| Esterification | RCOOH/H⁺ or RCOCl | Ester |

## Carbonyl Chemistry

The C=O group is electrophilic at C (δ+) and nucleophilic at O (δ−).

### Nucleophilic Addition to Aldehydes/Ketones

| Nucleophile | Reagent | Product |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Hydride (H⁻) | NaBH₄ (mild) | Alcohol |
| Hydride (H⁻) | LiAlH₄ (strong) | Alcohol (also reduces esters/acids) |
| Organolithium | RLi | Alcohol (tertiary) |
| Grignard | RMgX | Alcohol |
| Water | H₂O/H⁺ | Geminal diol (hydrate) |
| Alcohol | ROH/H⁺ | Hemiacetal → acetal |
| Amine | RNH₂ | Imine (Schiff base) |
| Cyanide | HCN | Cyanohydrin |

### Carboxylic Acid Derivatives (Reactivity Order)
Acid chloride > Anhydride > Ester ≈ Thioester > Amide > Carboxylate

All undergo **nucleophilic acyl substitution** (addition-elimination):
Nucleophile attacks C=O → tetrahedral intermediate → leaving group departs

### Enolate Chemistry
α-carbon (adjacent to C=O) is acidic; deprotonation gives enolate (stabilized by resonance)

- **Aldol reaction**: enolate + aldehyde → β-hydroxy carbonyl; dehydration → α,β-unsaturated carbonyl
- **Claisen condensation**: ester + enolate (ester) → β-ketoester; requires 2 ester equivalents
- **Michael addition**: enolate + Michael acceptor (α,β-unsaturated carbonyl) → 1,4-addition product
- **Mannich reaction**: aldehyde + ketone + amine → β-amino carbonyl

## Amines

### Basicity
Amine basicity: pKb ≈ 3–4 (aliphatic), ≈ 8–9 (aromatic)  
Order: 3° > 2° > 1° > NH₃ > aromatic amines (resonance delocalizes lone pair)

### Reactions
- **Alkylation**: RNH₂ + R'X → R(R')NH → over-alkylation problem
- **Acylation**: RNH₂ + R'COCl → R'CONHR (amide)
- **Diazotization**: Ar-NH₂ + NaNO₂/HCl → Ar-N₂⁺ (diazonium salt); useful in synthesis
- **Reductive amination**: R=O + NH₃ or RNH₂ + NaCNBH₃ → amine

## Polymers

### Addition (Chain-Growth) Polymers
Monomer: usually alkenes; free radical, cationic, or anionic initiation

| Monomer | Polymer | Name | Use |
|---------|---------|------|-----|
| Ethylene | –[CH₂CH₂]ₙ– | Polyethylene (PE) | Bags, bottles |
| Propylene | –[CH₂CH(CH₃)]ₙ– | Polypropylene (PP) | Containers, fiber |
| Styrene | –[CH₂CH(Ph)]ₙ– | Polystyrene (PS) | Foam, packaging |
| Vinyl chloride | –[CH₂CHCl]ₙ– | PVC | Pipe, flooring |
| Tetrafluoroethylene | –[CF₂CF₂]ₙ– | PTFE (Teflon) | Non-stick, seals |
| Acrylonitrile | –[CH₂CHCN]ₙ– | Polyacrylonitrile | Carbon fiber precursor |

### Condensation (Step-Growth) Polymers
Two monomers react with loss of small molecule (H₂O, HCl)

| Monomers | Polymer | Name | Use |
|---------|---------|------|-----|
| Diol + dicarboxylic acid | Polyester | PET, Dacron | Bottles, fiber |
| Diamine + diacid chloride | Polyamide | Nylon, Kevlar | Fiber, bulletproof |
| Diisocyanate + diol | Polyurethane | PU | Foam, coatings |
| Bisphenol A + phosgene | Polycarbonate | PC | Glasses, CDs |

### Rubber
- Natural rubber: polyisoprene (cis-1,4); vulcanization with S creates crosslinks → harder, more durable
- Neoprene: polychloroprene; chemical/oil resistant

## Spectroscopy in Organic Chemistry (Brief Overview)

| Technique | What it reveals |
|-----------|----------------|
| IR | Functional groups (characteristic bond stretches) |
| ¹H NMR | H environments; chemical shift, splitting, integration |
| ¹³C NMR | Carbon environments; number of unique carbons |
| Mass spectrometry | Molecular mass, fragmentation pattern |
| UV-Vis | Conjugated systems, aromatic rings (chromophores) |
