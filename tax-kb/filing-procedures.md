# Filing Procedures (2023, 2024, 2025)

> **Sources:** IRS Publications 17, 505, 509; IRS Forms 1040, 4868; IRC Sections 6651, 6654

---

## Filing Deadlines

### Individual Tax Returns (Form 1040)

| Tax Year | Filing Deadline | Extension Deadline |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2023 | April 15, 2024 | October 15, 2024 |
| 2024 | April 15, 2025 | October 15, 2025 |
| 2025 | April 15, 2026 | October 15, 2026 |

If the deadline falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, it moves to the next business day.

### Business Returns

| Entity Type | Form | Due Date | Extended Due Date |
|-------------|------|----------|-------------------|
| Sole proprietorship | Schedule C (with 1040) | April 15 | October 15 |
| Partnership | Form 1065 | March 15 | September 15 |
| S Corporation | Form 1120-S | March 15 | September 15 |
| C Corporation | Form 1120 | April 15 | October 15 |
| Trust / Estate | Form 1041 | April 15 | September 30 |
| Tax-exempt org | Form 990 | May 15 | November 15 |

### Other Important Dates
- **FBAR (FinCEN 114):** April 15 (automatic extension to October 15)
- **Form 3520 (foreign trust):** April 15 (extension to October 15)
- **Form 5471 (foreign corporation):** Filed with income tax return
- **Form 8938 (FATCA):** Filed with income tax return

## Extension Rules (Form 4868)

### Individual Extensions
- **Automatic 6-month extension** to file (April 15 to October 15)
- File Form 4868 by April 15 (or make a payment designated as an extension)
- **An extension to file is NOT an extension to pay** -- estimated taxes owed must be paid by April 15
- No reason needed; extension is automatic upon timely filing of Form 4868
- Penalties and interest accrue on unpaid taxes from the original due date

### How to File an Extension
1. **E-file Form 4868** through IRS Free File or tax software
2. **Pay electronically** via IRS Direct Pay or EFTPS (payment alone counts as extension request)
3. **Mail Form 4868** with payment (if any)

### Special Extension Situations
- **U.S. citizens/residents abroad:** Automatic 2-month extension to June 15 (interest still runs from April 15)
- **Military in combat zone:** Deadline extended by time in combat zone plus 180 days
- **Disaster relief:** IRS may extend deadlines for federally declared disaster areas

## Estimated Tax Payment Due Dates

### Quarterly Schedule (Calendar-Year Taxpayers)

| Quarter | Income Period | Due Date |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Q1 | January 1 - March 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | April 1 - May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | June 1 - August 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | September 1 - December 31 | January 15 (next year) |

### Who Must Pay Estimated Taxes
Generally required if you expect to owe $1,000 or more when your return is filed, after subtracting withholding and refundable credits.

### Safe Harbor Rules (Avoid Underpayment Penalty)
No penalty if you pay at least the lesser of:
1. **90%** of the current year's tax liability, OR
2. **100%** of the prior year's tax liability (on a return covering a full 12 months)
   - **110%** if prior year AGI exceeded $150,000 ($75,000 for MFS)

### Annualized Income Installment Method
- If income is not received evenly throughout the year (e.g., large Q4 gain), you can use Form 2210, Schedule AI to calculate required payments based on income received in each period
- Can reduce or eliminate penalties for uneven income

## Penalties

### Failure to File Penalty (IRC Section 6651(a)(1))
- **5% per month** (or partial month) of the unpaid tax, up to **25% maximum**
- If more than 60 days late: minimum penalty is the **lesser of $485 (2024 returns) / $510 (2025 returns) or 100% of tax owed**
- Reduced to **0.5% per month** if a valid extension was filed and at least 90% of tax was paid by the due date

### Failure to Pay Penalty (IRC Section 6651(a)(2))
- **0.5% per month** (or partial month) of the unpaid tax, up to **25% maximum**
- Increases to **1% per month** if IRS sends notice of intent to levy and payment not made within 10 days
- Reduced to **0.25% per month** if an approved installment agreement is in effect

### Combined Penalties
- If both failure to file and failure to pay apply in the same month, the failure to file penalty is reduced by the failure to pay penalty
- Maximum combined penalty is still 5% per month for the first 5 months

### Underpayment of Estimated Tax Penalty (IRC Section 6654)
- Calculated at the federal short-term rate plus 3 percentage points (changes quarterly)
- Applied to the shortfall for each quarter
- 2024 penalty rate: approximately 8%
- 2025 penalty rate: approximately 7-8% (varies by quarter)
- No maximum; calculated as interest on the underpayment from due date to payment date
- Can be waived for casualty, disaster, or other unusual circumstances

### Interest on Underpayments
- Federal short-term rate + 3% (compounded daily)
- Runs from the original due date until paid
- Cannot be waived (even if penalties are abated)

## Which Form to Use

### Form 1040 (U.S. Individual Income Tax Return)
- Used by virtually all individual taxpayers
- Form 1040-SR: Same as Form 1040 but with larger print, designed for age 65+

### Common Schedules

| Schedule | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Schedule 1 | Additional income (alimony, business income, unemployment) and adjustments to income (student loan interest, IRA deduction, SE tax deduction) |
| Schedule 2 | Additional taxes (AMT, excess premium tax credit repayment, SE tax, household employment tax) |
| Schedule 3 | Additional credits (foreign tax credit, education credits, general business credit) and payments (estimated tax, extension payment) |
| Schedule A | Itemized deductions |
| Schedule B | Interest and ordinary dividends (over $1,500) |
| Schedule C | Profit or loss from business (sole proprietorship) |
| Schedule D | Capital gains and losses |
| Schedule E | Supplemental income (rental, partnership, S corp, trusts) |
| Schedule F | Profit or loss from farming |
| Schedule H | Household employment taxes (nanny tax) |
| Schedule SE | Self-employment tax |

## E-Filing Requirements

### Mandatory E-Filing
- Tax return preparers who file 11 or more individual returns must e-file (reduced from 200 threshold, effective 2024)
- Business returns: Required for most businesses with assets of $10 million+
- Individuals: E-filing not mandatory but strongly encouraged

### E-Filing Options
- **IRS Free File:** Free for AGI $84,000 or less (2025); guided tax software
- **IRS Direct File:** Free IRS-provided option (available in select states, expanding)
- **Commercial tax software:** TurboTax, H&R Block, TaxAct, FreeTaxUSA, etc.
- **Tax professional:** CPA, Enrolled Agent, attorney
- **VITA/TCE:** Free tax preparation for qualifying individuals (low-income, elderly, military)

### E-Filing Benefits
- Faster processing (typically 21 days for refund vs. 6+ weeks for paper)
- Immediate confirmation of receipt
- Fewer errors (software catches math errors)
- Required for certain credits and forms

## Identity Protection PIN (IP PIN)

- 6-digit number assigned by the IRS to prevent identity theft-related tax fraud
- Must be entered on the tax return for it to be accepted
- Available to all taxpayers (voluntary enrollment at irs.gov/ippin)
- New IP PIN issued each year in January
- If you don't receive or lose your IP PIN, can retrieve online or call IRS

## Statute of Limitations

### Assessment (IRS Can Audit/Assess Additional Tax)

| Situation | Time Limit |
|-----------|-----------|
| Normal return | 3 years from filing date or due date (whichever is later) |
| Substantial understatement (>25% of gross income) | 6 years |
| Fraud or willful tax evasion | No limit |
| Failure to file a return | No limit |
| Foreign income (>$5,000 omitted) | 6 years |
| Basis overstatement (>25%) | 6 years |

### Refund Claims (Taxpayer Can Claim Refund)

| Situation | Time Limit |
|-----------|-----------|
| From filing date | 3 years |
| From payment date | 2 years |
| Whichever is later applies | |

### Other Limitations
- IRS must collect assessed tax within 10 years (Collection Statute Expiration Date)
- Signing Form 872 extends the assessment period (by agreement)
- Filing an amended return does not restart the 3-year clock for the original return

## Amended Returns (Form 1040-X)

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Form | 1040-X |
| E-filing available | Yes (for 2021 and later tax years) |
| Deadline | Within 3 years of original filing or 2 years of tax payment (whichever is later) |
| Processing time | Up to 16 weeks (or longer during peak) |
| When to file | Errors in income, filing status, deductions, or credits |
| Does NOT need amending | Math errors (IRS corrects), missing forms (IRS will request) |

## Payment Options

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| IRS Direct Pay | Free; bank account; irs.gov/payments |
| EFTPS | Free; requires enrollment; scheduled payments |
| Credit/Debit Card | Convenience fee (1.85-1.98% credit, $2.50-$2.69 debit) |
| Check/Money Order | Mail with payment voucher (Form 1040-V) |
| Cash | At participating retail partners (PayNearMe) |
| Installment Agreement | Form 9465; user fee $22-$225; interest + 0.25% penalty continue |
| Offer in Compromise | Settle for less than owed; Form 656; $205 application fee |

### Installment Agreement Thresholds
- **Guaranteed:** Owe $10,000 or less (after credits/payments); must file and pay on time for past 5 years
- **Streamlined:** Owe $50,000 or less; up to 72 months to pay; no financial statement required
- **Non-streamlined:** Over $50,000; requires financial disclosure (Form 433-A/F)
