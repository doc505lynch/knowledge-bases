## New Mexico Standard Deduction / Itemized Deduction Rules

New Mexico does **not** have its own standard deduction. Instead:

- NM taxable income starts with **federal adjusted gross income (AGI)**
- NM then applies its own modifications (additions and subtractions)
- Taxpayers do NOT separately itemize on their NM return -- the federal AGI starting point already reflects the choice of standard vs. itemized deductions at the federal level
- **Low-and-middle-income exemption:** An exemption of up to $2,500 per qualifying exemption is available based on AGI

## New Mexico Credits and Deductions

### NM Working Families Tax Credit (NM EITC)

| Tax Year | Credit Rate (% of Federal EITC) |
|----------|---------------------------------|
| 2023 | 25% of federal EITC |
| 2024 | 25% of federal EITC |
| 2025 | 25% of federal EITC |

- Refundable credit
- Must qualify for and claim the federal EITC
- Example: If federal EITC is $4,000, NM credit = $1,000

### NM Child Income Tax Credit (Effective 2023)

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Credit amount per qualifying child | Up to $600 (income-dependent) |
| Qualifying child age | Under 6 |
| Refundable | Yes |
| Income thresholds | Phases out at higher income levels |

### NM Low-Income Comprehensive Tax Rebate (LICTR)

- Available to NM residents with modified gross income below certain thresholds
- Amount depends on income and number of exemptions
- Claimed on Schedule PIT-RC
- Must file a NM return to receive the rebate

### NM Property Tax Rebate

- Available to NM residents who owned and occupied a primary residence
- Income-based (lower income = larger rebate)
- Claimed on Form RPD-41348 (filed with PIT-1)
- Maximum property tax rebate varies by income level
- Age 65+ may qualify for additional rebate amounts

### NM Medical Care Expense Deduction

- Taxpayers age 65+ may deduct up to $3,000 in unreimbursed medical expenses for themselves, spouse, or dependents
- This is a deduction from NM taxable income, separate from the federal medical expense deduction

### NM Solar Market Development Tax Credit

- 10% of purchase and installation costs of a solar energy system
- Maximum credit: $6,000 per solar thermal system, $6,000 per photovoltaic system
- Unused credit carries forward up to 10 years
- Subject to annual cap allocation by the Energy, Minerals, and Natural Resources Department

### NM Angel Investment Credit

- 25% of qualified investment in NM high-technology or manufacturing businesses
- Maximum credit: $62,500 per investment per year
- 40% for investments in rural areas or underserved communities

### NM Sustainable Building Tax Credit

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Purpose | Constructing or renovating sustainable residential/commercial buildings |
| Certification | LEED Silver or higher (U.S. Green Building Council) or Build Green NM |
| Credit basis | Determined by building's square footage and certification level |
| Refundable | No (nonrefundable, carries forward) |

### NM Film Production Tax Credit

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Base credit | **25%** of qualified direct production expenditures |
| Series TV / standalone pilots uplift | +5% (6+ episodes, $50K+ NM budget/episode) |
| Qualified production facility (QPF) uplift | +5% (certified soundstages/standing sets) |
| Filming Uplift Zone (rural) uplift | +10% (60+ miles from Santa Fe/ABQ City Halls) |
| **Maximum stackable rate** | **40%** |

- Film Partners (NBC Universal, Netflix, 828 Productions): 25-35%, separate uncapped fund
- FY2025 annual fund cap: **$130 million** (rising to $160M by FY2029)
- Qualified spend: direct production expenditures subject to NM taxation, NM resident wages, nonresident performing artist wages, limited nonresident below-the-line wages

### Other NM Credits
- **Veterans' credit:** $4,000 exemption from NM income for certain veterans
- **Blind/disabled credit:** Additional exemption amounts

## New Mexico Gross Receipts Tax (GRT)

**Important:** New Mexico does NOT have a traditional sales tax. Instead, it imposes a **Gross Receipts Tax (GRT)** on the seller/service provider, which is typically passed on to the buyer.

### State Base Rate

| Period | State GRT Rate |
|--------|---------------|
| 2023 | 5.00% |
| 2024 | 5.00% |
| 2025 (Jan-Jun) | 4.875% |
| 2025 (Jul+) | 4.75% |

### Combined Rates (State + County + Municipal)
- Range: approximately 5.125% to 8.6875% depending on location
- **Albuquerque (2025):** approximately 7.875% combined
- **Santa Fe (2025):** approximately 8.4375% combined
- **Las Cruces (2025):** approximately 8.3125% combined

### Key GRT Differences from Sales Tax
- Tax is on the **seller's gross receipts**, not on the buyer's purchase
- Many **services** are taxable (unlike most states' sales taxes)
- The seller owes the tax regardless of whether it is passed to the buyer
- Deductions (not exemptions) reduce the taxable base
- Common deductions: sales to federal government, wholesale sales, prescription drugs, most food for home consumption (exempt since 2025)

### Food GRT Exemption
- **Effective January 1, 2025:** Groceries (food for home consumption) are exempt from state GRT
- Local GRT on food was phased out starting July 1, 2024
- This does NOT apply to prepared food, restaurant meals, or alcohol

## New Mexico Property Tax

### Overview
- NM property taxes are administered at the county level
- Tax rates are expressed in mills (1 mill = $1 per $1,000 of assessed value)
- **Effective property tax rate:** Approximately 0.61% of owner-occupied housing value (among the lowest in the U.S.)

### Assessment and Valuation
- **Residential property:** Assessed at 1/3 of market value
- **Non-residential property:** Assessed at 1/3 of market value
- **Annual increase cap:** Assessed value of residential property cannot increase more than 3% per year (unless sold or improved)
- **Head of family exemption:** $2,000 off assessed value
- **Veterans' exemption:** $4,000 off assessed value for honorably discharged veterans
- **Disabled veterans' exemption:** Complete exemption on first $100,000 of value (100% disabled)

### Property Tax Rebate (State Income Tax)
- NM allows a rebate of property taxes paid, claimed on the state income tax return
- Income-based; most beneficial for lower-income homeowners

## NM Capital Gains Deduction

### 40% Deduction on NM Business Capital Gains
- Taxpayers may deduct **40%** of capital gains from the sale of a **New Mexico business**
- Applies to gains on assets held for **1+ year** attributable to NM-based businesses
- Maximum qualifying gain: up to **$1,000,000**
- Maximum deduction: **$400,000** (40% of $1M)

### General Capital Gains Deduction
- A separate deduction of up to **$2,500** is available for other capital gains
- Applies to standard investment gains not connected to a NM business sale

## NM Estimated Tax Rules

### When Required
You must make estimated payments if you expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in NM income tax after subtracting withholding and credits.

### Safe Harbor
Required annual payment is the lesser of:
- **90%** of the current year's tax liability, OR
- **100%** of the prior year's tax liability (full 12-month NM return filed)

### Payment Schedule

| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 | April 15 |
| Q2 | June 15 |
| Q3 | September 15 |
| Q4 | January 15 (following year) |

- Pay via **Taxpayer Access Point (TAP)** at https://tap.state.nm.us or by mail with Form PIT-ES
- Farmers earning 2/3+ gross income from farming may pay in a single payment by January 15

## NM Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTE) Election

NM allows pass-through entities (partnerships, S-corps, LLCs taxed as partnerships) to elect to pay income tax at the entity level, as a workaround for the federal $10,000 SALT deduction cap.

### How It Works
1. PTE elects annually to pay NM income tax on behalf of its owners
2. PTE deducts the tax payment on its **federal** return (bypassing the SALT cap)
3. Owners report their share of entity income on their NM return
4. Owners claim a **credit** for the PTE tax paid on their behalf

### Key Rules

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Election | Annual, made by filing PTE return |
| Binding | Binding on **all** owners of the electing PTE |
| Filing deadline | Original or extended due date of federal partnership/S-corp return |
| Estimated payments | Not required quarterly; full payment due by federal return due date |
| Best practice | Pay by **December 31** of the tax year for maximum federal deduction benefit |

### 2023 Changes (HB 368)
For tax years beginning in 2023+, NM shifted from income exemption to a **tax credit** system. PTE pays and deducts the tax; owners claim the credit.

## NM Cannabis Tax Provisions

### Cannabis Excise Tax (Adult-Use Only)

| Effective Date | Excise Tax Rate |
|----------------|----------------|
| July 1, 2022 | 12% |
| July 1, 2025 | 13% |
| Escalation | +1% per year until **18%** in July 2030 |

- Only **cannabis retailers** selling to end consumers pay the excise tax
- Wholesalers, manufacturers, and producers do not pay the excise tax
- **Medical cannabis** sales are **exempt** from the excise tax
- All cannabis sales (adult-use and medical) are also subject to local **GRT**

## New Mexico Retirement Income Exclusion

### Age 65+ Exemption

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum exemption per person | $8,000 |
| Qualifying age | 65 or older by end of tax year |
| Income limit | Based on AGI; higher income reduces the exemption |
| Applies to | All income types (pension, Social Security, etc.) |

### Social Security Income
- NM adopted a Social Security income exemption effective 2022
- **For TY 2023-2024:** Social Security benefits are exempt from NM income tax if AGI is below $100,000 (single) or $150,000 (MFJ)
- Benefits above those income thresholds are partially taxable at the state level
- **Effective 2025 (HB 252):** Full exemption of Social Security benefits regardless of income

### Railroad Retirement Benefits
- Exempt from NM income tax (as with federal)

### Military Retirement Pay
- Exempt from NM income tax (full exemption enacted in 2022)

## Filing Requirements and Deadlines

### Who Must File
- NM residents with income exceeding the federal filing threshold
- Non-residents and part-year residents with NM-source income
- Anyone who wants to claim NM credits or rebates

### Deadlines

| Tax Year | Filing Deadline | Extension Deadline |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| 2023 | April 15, 2024 | October 15, 2024 |
| 2024 | April 15, 2025 | October 15, 2025 |
| 2025 | April 15, 2026 | October 15, 2026 |

### Key Forms
| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| PIT-1 | NM Personal Income Tax Return |
| PIT-ADJ | Schedule of additions and deductions |
| PIT-RC | Rebate and credit schedule |
| RPD-41348 | Property tax rebate claim |
| PIT-B | Income allocation (non-residents/part-year) |

### Extension
- Filing a federal extension (Form 4868) automatically extends the NM filing deadline
- No separate NM extension form is needed if federal extension is filed
- Estimated tax payments are still due by April 15

## NM Community Property Rules

New Mexico is a **community property state**. This means:
- Income earned during marriage is generally split 50/50 between spouses
- Affects MFS filers: each spouse reports half of community income
- Applies to wages, business income, investment income earned during marriage
- Separate property (owned before marriage, gifts, inheritances) is not community property
- Must allocate community vs. separate income when filing MFS
