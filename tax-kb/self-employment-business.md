# Self-Employment and Business Taxes (2023, 2024, 2025)

> **Sources:** IRS Publications 334, 535, 587, 946; IRS Schedule C, SE; IRC Sections 179, 199A, 168(k)

---

## Self-Employment Tax

### Tax Rate and Calculation

| Component | Rate | 2023-2025 |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Social Security (OASDI) | 12.4% | On first $160,200 (2023), $168,600 (2024), $176,100 (2025) |
| Medicare | 2.9% | On all net SE income |
| Additional Medicare Tax | 0.9% | On SE income over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ) |
| **Total SE tax rate** | **15.3%** | On net SE income up to Social Security wage base |

### Social Security Wage Base

| Year | Wage Base |
|------|-----------|
| 2023 | $160,200 |
| 2024 | $168,600 |
| 2025 | $176,100 |

### Calculation Steps
1. Start with net profit from Schedule C (or Schedule F for farming)
2. Multiply by 92.35% (0.9235) to get the SE tax base
3. Apply 15.3% on the first [wage base] amount, 2.9% on the remainder
4. Add 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on amounts over the threshold

### SE Tax Deduction
- **50% of SE tax** is deductible as an above-the-line deduction (Schedule 1, Part II)
- This reduces AGI but does NOT reduce SE income for SE tax calculation purposes
- Also referred to as the "employer-equivalent portion"

## Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction -- Section 199A

### Overview
- Deduction of up to **20%** of qualified business income from pass-through entities (sole proprietorships, partnerships, S corporations, some trusts/estates)
- Plus 20% of qualified REIT dividends and qualified PTP income
- This is a deduction from taxable income, not from AGI

### Taxable Income Thresholds

| Filing Status | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|---------------|------|------|------|
| Single / HoH / MFS threshold | $182,100 | $191,950 | $197,300 |
| MFJ threshold | $364,200 | $383,900 | $394,600 |
| Phase-in range (Single) | $182,100 - $232,100 | $191,950 - $241,950 | $197,300 - $247,300 |
| Phase-in range (MFJ) | $364,200 - $464,200 | $383,900 - $483,900 | $394,600 - $494,600 |

### Rules by Income Level

**Below threshold:** Full 20% QBI deduction with no limitations.

**Within phase-in range:**
- The W-2 wage/UBIA limitation begins to phase in
- SSTB (Specified Service Trade or Business) income begins to phase out

**Above phase-in range:**
- QBI deduction limited to the GREATER of:
  - 50% of W-2 wages paid by the business, OR
  - 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of the unadjusted basis immediately after acquisition (UBIA) of qualified property
- SSTBs get NO QBI deduction above the phase-in range

### Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs)
- Health, law, accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, athletics, financial services, brokerage, any business where the principal asset is the reputation or skill of employees/owners
- Engineering and architecture are specifically EXCLUDED from SSTB (they qualify for QBI)

### QBI Deduction Calculation (Simplified)
- Lesser of: (a) 20% of QBI, or (b) 20% of taxable income before QBI deduction minus net capital gains

## Business Expense Deductions

### Home Office Deduction

**Simplified Method:**
| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Rate | $5 per square foot |
| Maximum area | 300 sq ft |
| Maximum deduction | $1,500 |

**Regular Method:**
- Actual expenses (mortgage interest/rent, utilities, insurance, repairs, depreciation) prorated by business-use percentage
- Business-use percentage = square footage of office / total home square footage
- No limit on deduction amount (but limited to gross income from business use)
- Excess can be carried forward

**Requirements (both methods):**
- Regular and exclusive use for business
- Principal place of business, or place to meet clients/customers, or separate structure
- Employees: Must be for employer's convenience (not just working from home by choice)

### Vehicle / Auto Expenses

**Standard Mileage Rate:**

| Year | Business Rate | Medical/Moving Rate | Charity Rate |
|------|---------------|--------------------:|-------------:|
| 2023 | 65.5 cents/mile | 22 cents/mile | 14 cents/mile |
| 2024 | 67 cents/mile | 21 cents/mile | 14 cents/mile |
| 2025 | 70 cents/mile | 21 cents/mile | 14 cents/mile |

**Actual Expense Method:** Track all actual costs (gas, oil, repairs, insurance, registration, depreciation/lease payments) and multiply by business-use percentage.

- Cannot use standard mileage if: used 5+ vehicles simultaneously, claimed depreciation other than straight-line, claimed Section 179 or bonus depreciation, or are a rural mail carrier
- Must choose method in first year vehicle is used for business; can switch in later years (with restrictions)

### Meals and Entertainment

| Expense Type | 2023-2025 |
|-------------|-----------|
| Business meals (with client/business associate) | 50% deductible |
| Meals while traveling for business | 50% deductible |
| Employee meals for convenience of employer | 50% deductible |
| Entertainment expenses | NOT deductible (eliminated by TCJA) |
| Holiday parties / company picnics (all employees) | 100% deductible |

**Note:** The temporary 100% deduction for restaurant meals (2021-2022) expired at the end of 2022. Starting 2023, the standard 50% limit applies.

### Travel Expenses
- Transportation (airfare, train, bus, rental car)
- Lodging
- Meals (50% limit)
- Incidentals (tips, laundry, etc.)
- Must be "away from home" overnight for business purposes
- Per diem rates available as an alternative (see IRS Publication 1542)

## Depreciation

### Section 179 Expensing

| Detail | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|--------|------|------|------|
| Maximum deduction | $1,160,000 | $1,220,000 | $1,250,000 |
| Phase-out threshold (investment limit) | $2,890,000 | $3,050,000 | $3,130,000 |
| SUV limit (GVW > 6,000 lbs) | $28,900 | $30,500 | $31,300 |

- Applies to tangible personal property, off-the-shelf software, certain improvements to nonresidential real property
- Cannot create or increase a net loss (limited to taxable income from active trades/businesses)
- Excess carries forward indefinitely

### Bonus Depreciation (Section 168(k))

| Year Property Placed in Service | Bonus Depreciation % | Long Production Period / Certain Aircraft |
|--------------------------------|---------------------|------------------------------------------|
| 2023 | 80% | 100% |
| 2024 | 60% | 80% |
| 2025 | 40% | 60% |
| 2026 | 20% | 40% |
| 2027+ | 0% | 20% (2027), 0% (2028+) |

**IMPORTANT UPDATE -- "One, Big, Beautiful Bill" (July 2025):**
- Restores 100% bonus depreciation for property acquired AND placed in service after January 19, 2025
- Made permanent (no further phasedown for qualifying property)
- Property acquired before January 20, 2025 follows the original phasedown schedule above
- Taxpayers may elect 40% (or 60% for long production period property) instead of 100%

## Estimated Tax Payments

### Due Dates (Calendar-Year Taxpayers)

| Quarter | Income Period | Due Date |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Q1 | Jan 1 - Mar 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | Apr 1 - May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | Jun 1 - Aug 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | Sep 1 - Dec 31 | January 15 (following year) |

If due date falls on weekend/holiday, next business day.

### Safe Harbor Rules (Avoid Underpayment Penalty)
You owe no penalty if you pay at least the lesser of:
1. **90%** of current year's tax liability, OR
2. **100%** of prior year's tax liability (110% if prior year AGI exceeded $150,000 / $75,000 MFS)

### Payment Methods
- IRS Direct Pay (bank account) -- irs.gov/payments
- EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System)
- Credit/debit card (convenience fee applies)
- Check/money order with Form 1040-ES voucher

## 1099 Reporting Thresholds

### 1099-NEC (Nonemployee Compensation)

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Reporting threshold | $600 |
| Due to IRS | January 31 |
| Due to recipient | January 31 |

### 1099-K (Payment Card and Third-Party Network Transactions)

| Year | Reporting Threshold |
|------|--------------------|
| 2023 | $20,000 AND 200+ transactions (original threshold; $600 threshold delayed) |
| 2024 | $5,000 (transitional threshold; no transaction count requirement) |
| 2025 | $2,500 (planned phase-in; may be superseded) |

**IMPORTANT UPDATE:** The "One, Big, Beautiful Bill" (July 2025) retroactively reverted the 1099-K threshold to $20,000 AND 200+ transactions (the pre-American Rescue Plan level), effective for all years. The $600 threshold from ARPA was never implemented.

### 1099-MISC
- $600 for rents, prizes/awards, other income, attorney payments
- $10 for royalties
- $5,000 for direct sales of consumer products

## Schedule C Basics

### Who Files Schedule C
- Sole proprietors
- Single-member LLCs (disregarded entities)
- Statutory employees
- Independent contractors

### Key Line Items
- **Part I:** Gross income (1099-NEC income, cash receipts, returns/allowances)
- **Part II:** Expenses (advertising, car/truck, contract labor, depreciation, insurance, interest, office expense, rent, repairs, supplies, taxes, travel, meals, utilities, wages, other)
- **Part III:** Cost of Goods Sold (if applicable)
- **Part IV:** Vehicle information (if claiming auto expenses)
- **Part V:** Other expenses (detailed list)

### Record-Keeping Requirements
- Keep receipts and records for all business income and expenses
- Maintain a separate business bank account (recommended, not required for sole proprietors)
- Retain records for at least 3 years from the filing date (6 years if underreporting income by 25%+)
- Vehicle log: date, destination, business purpose, miles driven
