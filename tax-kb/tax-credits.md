## Child Tax Credit (CTC)

### Credit Amounts

| Detail | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|--------|------|------|------|
| Maximum CTC per qualifying child | $2,000 | $2,000 | $2,000 |
| Refundable portion (Additional CTC / ACTC) | $1,600 | $1,700 | $1,700 |
| Qualifying child age | Under 17 | Under 17 | Under 17 |

### Income Phaseouts

| Filing Status | Phaseout Begins | All Years 2023-2025 |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Single / HoH / MFS | $200,000 | Same |
| Married Filing Jointly | $400,000 | Same |

The credit is reduced by $50 for each $1,000 (or fraction thereof) of MAGI above the threshold.

### Other Dependent Credit
- $500 non-refundable credit for dependents who don't qualify for the CTC (e.g., children 17+, qualifying relatives)
- Same income phaseout thresholds as CTC

### Eligibility Requirements

- Age requirement (no children): 25-64 (expanded to 19+ under certain conditions)
- Cannot be a qualifying child of another taxpayer

## Child and Dependent Care Credit

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum qualifying expenses: 1 child/dependent | $3,000 |
| Maximum qualifying expenses: 2+ children/dependents | $6,000 |
| Credit percentage range | 20% - 35% of expenses |
| Maximum credit: 1 child | $600 - $1,050 |
| Maximum credit: 2+ children | $1,200 - $2,100 |

### Credit Percentage by AGI
- AGI up to $15,000: 35%
- Decreases by 1% for each $2,000 (or fraction) of AGI above $15,000
- Minimum percentage: 20% (for AGI over $43,000)

**Note:** This is a non-refundable credit. The temporary enhancements from 2021 (ARPA) expired.

### Qualifying Expenses
- Day care, babysitter, nanny, nursery school, preschool
- Before/after school care
- Summer day camp (NOT overnight camp)
- Care for a disabled spouse or dependent

| Maximum credit per student | $2,500 |

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum credit per return | $2,000 |
| Refundable | No |

### Credit Rates

| Credit Rate | 2023 Single/MFS | 2023 HoH | 2023 MFJ |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|----------|
| 50% | $0 - $21,750 | $0 - $32,625 | $0 - $43,500 |
| 20% | $21,751 - $23,750 | $32,626 - $35,625 | $43,501 - $47,500 |
| 10% | $23,751 - $36,500 | $35,626 - $54,750 | $47,501 - $73,000 |

| Credit Rate | 2024 Single/MFS | 2024 HoH | 2024 MFJ |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|----------|
| 50% | $0 - $23,000 | $0 - $34,500 | $0 - $46,000 |
| 20% | $23,001 - $25,000 | $34,501 - $37,500 | $46,001 - $50,000 |
| 10% | $25,001 - $38,250 | $37,501 - $57,375 | $50,001 - $76,500 |

| Credit Rate | 2025 Single/MFS | 2025 HoH | 2025 MFJ |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|----------|
| 50% | $0 - $23,750 | $0 - $35,625 | $0 - $47,500 |

## Premium Tax Credit (PTC) -- ACA Marketplace

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Available to | Marketplace health insurance purchasers |
| Income range | 100% - 400% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL) |
| Enhanced PTC (ARP extension) | No income cap through 2025; premiums capped at 8.5% of income |

**Note:** The enhanced premium tax credit, originally from the American Rescue Plan Act (2021) and extended by the Inflation Reduction Act through 2025, removes the 400% FPL income cliff. Taxpayers above 400% FPL still get the credit as long as their benchmark premium exceeds 8.5% of household income. This enhancement was set to expire after 2025 but was extended through 2026 by the "One, Big, Beautiful Bill."

### How It Works
- Based on household income relative to FPL
- Can be taken in advance (reducing monthly premiums) or claimed at filing
- Must file Form 8962 to reconcile advance payments
- Must file a return even if not otherwise required to, if advance PTC was received

| Credit rate | 30% of costs |

## Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

| Detail | 2023-2025 |
|--------|-----------|
| Form | 1116 (or direct claim on Form 1040 if under $300/$600 MFJ) |
| Limit | Cannot exceed U.S. tax on foreign-source income |
| Carryback | 1 year |
| Carryforward | 10 years |
| Simplified election | Available if total creditable foreign tax is $300 or less ($600 MFJ), all from passive category, reported on Form 1099 or Schedule K-1 |

### Key Rules
- Choose between credit or deduction each year (credit is usually better)
- Must be a foreign tax on income (not VAT, customs, property tax)
- Separate limitation categories (general, passive, etc.)
- AMT foreign tax credit calculated separately
