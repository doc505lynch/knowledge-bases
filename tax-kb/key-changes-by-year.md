## New Provisions Taking Effect

### Effective 2023
- **RMD age increased to 73** (from 72) for those born 1951-1959 (SECURE 2.0)
- **RMD penalty reduced to 25%** (from 50%), 10% if corrected timely (SECURE 2.0)
- **Roth SEP and SIMPLE IRAs** allowed for the first time (SECURE 2.0)
- **Employer Roth matching/nonelective contributions** permitted (SECURE 2.0)
- **Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit** restructured: 30% rate, annual (not lifetime) limits, up to $3,200/year
- **Residential Clean Energy Credit** extended at 30% through 2032
- **New Clean Vehicle Credit** (Section 30D) restructured with manufacturing and sourcing requirements
- **Used Clean Vehicle Credit** (Section 25E) created: up to $4,000
- **Bonus depreciation drops to 80%** (from 100%)
- **Restaurant meals deduction returns to 50%** (temporary 100% for 2021-2022 expired)

### Effective 2024
- **529-to-Roth IRA rollovers** permitted: up to $35,000 lifetime (SECURE 2.0)
- **Roth 401(k) RMDs eliminated** for living account holders (SECURE 2.0)
- **Student loan employer match** allowed: employers can match student loan payments with retirement contributions (SECURE 2.0)
- **Emergency savings accounts** linked to retirement plans permitted (SECURE 2.0)
- **1099-K threshold reduced to $5,000** (transitional, down from $20,000/200 transactions)
- **Bonus depreciation drops to 60%**
- **Clean vehicle credit point-of-sale transfer** option available (dealers can provide credit at purchase)
- **EITC/CTC amounts adjusted** for inflation

### Effective 2025
- **Super catch-up contributions for ages 60-63:** Up to $11,250 for 401(k)/403(b), $5,250 for SIMPLE (SECURE 2.0)
- **Automatic enrollment required** for new 401(k) and 403(b) plans at 3-10% (SECURE 2.0)
- **Bonus depreciation drops to 40%** (then restored to 100% by OBBBA for property acquired after 1/19/2025)
- **Child Tax Credit: Refundable portion stays at $1,700**
- **New Mexico HB 252:** Restructured NM income tax brackets, lowered bottom rate to 1.5%, added 4.3% bracket
- **NM food GRT exemption** fully effective (state + local)
- **NM Social Security full exemption** regardless of income

## "One, Big, Beautiful Bill" (OBBBA) -- Signed July 4, 2025

Major tax provisions enacted:

### Extended/Made Permanent (from TCJA)
- **Seven income tax brackets** (10%-37%): Made permanent
- **Doubled standard deduction**: Made permanent
- **$0 personal exemption**: Made permanent
- **$10,000 SALT cap**: Increased to $40,000 for 2026 (with phaseout for high earners)
- **Qualified Business Income deduction** (Section 199A): Extended/expanded
- **Estate tax exemption**: Increased to $15,000,000 per individual for 2026+
- **Child Tax Credit**: Increased to $2,500 per child for 2026+, with higher refundable portion

### New Provisions
- **100% bonus depreciation restored** (retroactive to property acquired after 1/19/2025): Made permanent
- **No tax on tips**: Certain cash tips exempt from income tax (with limitations)
- **No tax on overtime**: Overtime pay deduction for certain workers
- **No tax on Social Security**: Deduction for Social Security benefits (effective 2025-2028)
- **Auto loan interest deduction**: For domestically manufactured vehicles
- **SALT cap increase**: $40,000 for 2026+ (phases down for AGI over $400,000 single / $500,000 MFJ)
- **1099-K threshold reverted**: Back to $20,000 and 200+ transactions (retroactive)

### Repealed/Phased Out
- **Clean energy credits**: Most IRA clean energy credits phased out or eliminated
- **EV tax credits**: Phased out after 2025 (with transition rules)
- **Energy efficient home improvement credit**: Terminated after 2025
- **Premium Tax Credit enhancements**: Extended through 2026, then expire

## TCJA Provisions -- Pre-OBBBA Sunset Schedule

Before the OBBBA was enacted, these TCJA provisions were scheduled to sunset after December 31, 2025:

| Provision | Would Have Reverted To |
|-----------|----------------------|
| 37% top rate | 39.6% top rate |
| Doubled standard deduction | Pre-2018 levels (~$7,000 single) |
| $0 personal exemption | ~$5,000+ per person |
| $10,000 SALT cap | No cap |
| 20% QBI deduction | Eliminated |
| Estate exemption ~$14M | ~$7M per individual |
| Child Tax Credit $2,000 | $1,000 |
| Pease limitation suspended | Reinstated |
| AMT exemption increase | Lower exemption |

**The OBBBA resolved most of these by making the TCJA provisions permanent or extending them, with modifications.**

## IRS Procedural Changes

### 2023
- IRS hired thousands of new employees under Inflation Reduction Act funding
- Free File income limit increased
- Digital asset question added to top of Form 1040 (prominent placement)

### 2024
- Tax preparer e-filing threshold reduced to 11 returns (from previous phase-in)
- IRS Direct File pilot launched in select states
- Improved identity verification for online accounts
- 1099-K threshold reduced to $5,000

### 2025
- IRS Direct File expanded to additional states
- Improved online account capabilities
- IP PIN available to all taxpayers for voluntary enrollment
- Enhanced scrutiny of high-income non-filers
- Form 1099-DA introduced for digital asset transactions from brokers (effective for 2025 transactions)
- Increased audit rates for high-income taxpayers, large corporations, and complex partnerships
