## Short-Term Capital Gains

Short-term capital gains (assets held 1 year or less) are taxed as **ordinary income** at the filer's marginal tax rate. There is no preferential rate for short-term gains.

---

## Qualified Dividends

Qualified dividends are taxed at the **same preferential rates as long-term capital gains** (0%, 15%, or 20%) using the same income thresholds shown above. To qualify, dividends must be paid by a U.S. corporation or a qualified foreign corporation, and the holding period requirement must be met (held for more than 60 days during the 121-day period around the ex-dividend date).

Non-qualified (ordinary) dividends are taxed at ordinary income rates.

---

## Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

The NIIT is an additional **3.8% tax** on the lesser of:
- Net investment income, OR
- The excess of modified AGI over the threshold amount

| Filing Status | MAGI Threshold |
|---------------|---------------|
| Single | $200,000 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $250,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $125,000 |
| Head of Household | $200,000 |

**Note:** These thresholds are NOT indexed for inflation and have remained the same since the NIIT was enacted in 2013. Net investment income includes interest, dividends, capital gains, rental and royalty income, and non-qualified annuities.

## Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

The AMT operates as a parallel tax system. Taxpayers must pay the higher of their regular tax or AMT.

### AMT Exemption Amounts

| Filing Status | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|---------------|------|------|------|
| Single / HoH | $81,300 | $85,700 | $88,100 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $126,500 | $133,300 | $137,000 |
| Married Filing Separately | $63,250 | $66,650 | $68,500 |

### AMT Exemption Phaseout Thresholds (begins at)

| Filing Status | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 |
|---------------|------|------|------|
| Single / HoH | $578,150 | $609,350 | $626,350 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $1,156,300 | $1,218,700 | $1,252,700 |
| Married Filing Separately | $578,150 | $609,350 | $626,350 |

The exemption phases out at a rate of 25 cents for every dollar of AMTI above the threshold.

### AMT Tax Rates

| Rate | 2023 (AMTI over) | 2024 (AMTI over) | 2025 (AMTI over) |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 26% | First dollars of AMTI | First dollars of AMTI | First dollars of AMTI |
| 28% | $220,700 | $232,600 | $239,100 |

For MFS filers, the 28% rate kicks in at half the above amounts.

## Key Year-Over-Year Changes

- **2023 to 2024:** Bracket thresholds increased approximately 5.4% (reflecting high inflation from CPI adjustments)
- **2024 to 2025:** Bracket thresholds increased approximately 2.8% (reflecting moderating inflation)
- All seven rates (10%-37%) remained unchanged across all three years
- NIIT thresholds remained unchanged (not indexed for inflation)
- AMT exemptions and thresholds increased with inflation each year

---

## TCJA Sunset Warning (2026)

Unless extended by Congress, the TCJA provisions expire after December 31, 2025. The "One, Big, Beautiful Bill" signed July 4, 2025 extended and modified many TCJA provisions. Key changes for 2026+:
- The seven-bracket structure with the 37% top rate is made permanent
- Bracket thresholds continue to be inflation-adjusted
