### SECURE 2.0 Super Catch-Up (Ages 60-63)
- **Effective:** Tax year 2025 and later
- Workers aged 60, 61, 62, or 63 can contribute the greater of $10,000 or 150% of the regular catch-up limit
- For 2025: 150% x $7,500 = $11,250
- This replaces (does not add to) the standard catch-up contribution
- Applies to 401(k), 403(b), and governmental 457(b) plans
- At age 64, reverts to the standard catch-up amount

### SECURE 2.0 Roth Catch-Up Requirement
- **Effective:** Delayed to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026
- Employees who earned $145,000+ in the prior year from that employer must make catch-up contributions on a Roth (after-tax) basis
- Employees earning under $145,000 can still make pre-tax catch-up contributions
- SIMPLE plans use a $145,000 threshold as well

### Backdoor Roth IRA
- High-income earners who exceed Roth IRA income limits can contribute to a non-deductible Traditional IRA and convert to Roth
- No income limit on conversions
- Pro-rata rule applies if you have pre-tax IRA balances (Form 8606)
- This strategy has not been eliminated (proposed in Build Back Better Act but not enacted)

- Employers with 25 or fewer employees can increase both the employee limit and catch-up limit by 10%

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

### RMD Starting Age

| Birth Year | RMD Beginning Age | Effective |
|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Before July 1, 1949 | 70.5 | Pre-SECURE Act |
| July 1, 1949 - Dec 31, 1950 | 72 | SECURE Act (2020) |
| Jan 1, 1951 - Dec 31, 1959 | 73 | SECURE 2.0 (2023) |
| Jan 1, 1960 and later | 75 | SECURE 2.0 (2033) |

### Key RMD Rules (2023-2025)
- **First RMD deadline:** April 1 of the year following the year you reach your RMD age
- **Subsequent RMDs:** December 31 of each year
- **Penalty for missed RMD:** 25% of the amount not distributed (reduced from 50% by SECURE 2.0)
  - Reduced to 10% if corrected within a "correction window" (generally by the end of the 2nd year following the year the RMD was due)
- **Roth IRAs:** No RMDs for the original owner (inherited Roth IRAs may have RMDs)
- **Roth 401(k):** No RMDs starting 2024 (SECURE 2.0 change; previously required RMDs)
- **Still-working exception:** 401(k) participants who are still working at the employer can delay RMDs from that employer's plan until retirement (does not apply to 5%+ owners)

### RMD Calculation
- Account balance as of December 31 of prior year / Life expectancy factor from IRS Uniform Lifetime Table
- If sole beneficiary is spouse more than 10 years younger, use Joint Life Expectancy Table

### Inherited IRA / 10-Year Rule
- **SECURE Act (deaths after 12/31/2019):** Most non-spouse beneficiaries must withdraw the entire inherited IRA within 10 years
- Exceptions (eligible designated beneficiaries): surviving spouse, minor children (until age 21), disabled/chronically ill, beneficiaries not more than 10 years younger than decedent
- **Annual RMDs within the 10-year window:** Required if the original owner had already begun taking RMDs (IRS final regulations, effective 2025)

## Roth Conversions

### Key Rules
- No income limit on Roth conversions
- Converted amount is taxable as ordinary income in the year of conversion (except previously taxed contributions)
- No 10% early withdrawal penalty on the converted amount (but earnings withdrawn within 5 years may be penalized)
- 5-year holding period for each conversion to avoid the 10% penalty on the converted amount if under age 59.5
- Cannot be "recharacterized" (undone) -- this was eliminated by the TCJA starting 2018
- Strategy: Convert in lower-income years to lock in lower tax rates
