# Genre-Specific Songwriting Guide

A comprehensive reference for writing lyrics tailored to specific genres, including structure, tempo, lyrical themes, vocabulary, rhyme density, vocal style, and instrument palette.

---

## Pop

**Typical Structure:** Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus
**Tempo Range:** 100-130 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Love, heartbreak, self-empowerment, dancing, youth, relationships, having fun
**Vocabulary:** Accessible, universal, conversational. Short words, simple sentences. Rarely obscure.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. ABAB or ABCB common. Chorus tends to rhyme more tightly than verses.
**Vocal Style:** Clean, polished, strong melodies. Belting in choruses. Ad-libs and runs common. Male or female.
**Instrument Palette:** Synths, programmed drums, bass, electric guitar, piano. Layered production. Electronic elements.
**Suno Style Tags:** `pop, catchy, polished, upbeat, female/male vocals, synth-pop, dance-pop, electropop`

**Key Tips:**
- The chorus must be instantly memorable
- Pre-choruses are almost mandatory in modern pop
- Repetition is key — the hook should appear 4-6 times minimum
- Keep lyrics singalong-friendly
- "Less is more" lyrically — clarity over complexity

## Rock

**Typical Structure:** Intro (riff) → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Solo → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 110-145 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Rebellion, freedom, love, angst, social commentary, identity, partying, road life
**Vocabulary:** Direct, raw, energetic. Can be poetic or blunt depending on subgenre.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate to high. AABB and ABAB common. Internal rhymes in punk/alt-rock.
**Vocal Style:** Powerful, gritty, raspy. Range from melodic to shouted. Often male-dominated but not exclusively.
**Instrument Palette:** Electric guitar (distorted), bass guitar, drums, occasional piano/organ. Guitar solos important.
**Suno Style Tags:** `rock, electric guitar, driving drums, raw, energetic, guitar-driven, power chords`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Classic Rock:** Blues-influenced, extended solos, 70s warmth — `classic rock, 70s, blues-rock, analog`
- **Alternative/Indie Rock:** Atmospheric, jangly guitars, introspective — `indie rock, alternative, jangly guitars, atmospheric`
- **Hard Rock:** Heavy riffs, aggressive, stadium energy — `hard rock, heavy riffs, aggressive, stadium`
- **Punk Rock:** Fast, short, raw, political — `punk rock, fast, raw, aggressive, 180 BPM`
- **Grunge:** Dark, distorted, disaffected — `grunge, distorted, 90s, brooding, flannel`
- **Progressive Rock:** Complex, long-form, time changes — `progressive rock, complex, odd time signatures, epic`

## Hip-Hop / Rap

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse 1 → Hook → Verse 2 → Hook → Verse 3 → Hook → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 80-100 BPM (boom bap), 130-150 BPM half-time (trap)
**Lyrical Themes:** Street life, success, struggle, flexing, social justice, storytelling, braggadocio, introspection
**Vocabulary:** Slang-heavy, culturally specific, wordplay-dense. Multi-syllable rhymes valued. Punchlines.
**Rhyme Density:** Very high. Complex rhyme schemes, internal rhymes, multi-syllable rhymes, assonance.
**Vocal Style:** Rap delivery with varying flows. Melodic rap trending. Ad-libs ("yeah," "what," "skrrt"). Sometimes sung hooks.
**Instrument Palette:** 808 bass, hi-hats (rolls), snares, synths, samples, piano loops, strings. Minimalist beats or layered.
**Suno Style Tags:** `hip-hop, rap, 808 bass, hi-hat rolls, trap, boom bap, lyrical rap`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Trap:** 808s, hi-hat rolls, dark synths, half-time feel — `trap, dark, 808s, hi-hat rolls, 140 BPM`
- **Boom Bap:** Sample-based, jazzy, classic feel — `boom bap, 90s hip-hop, jazz samples, vinyl`
- **Conscious Rap:** Thoughtful, socially aware, lyrical — `conscious hip-hop, lyrical, soulful, introspective`
- **Drill:** Dark, aggressive, sliding 808s — `drill, dark, aggressive, sliding bass, UK drill`
- **Lo-fi Hip-Hop:** Chill, jazzy, study beats — `lo-fi hip-hop, chill, jazzy, tape hiss, mellow`
- **Melodic Rap:** Sung melodies over trap beats — `melodic rap, auto-tune, emotional, sung rap`

**Key Tips:**
- Flow (rhythmic delivery) is as important as the words
- Bars are typically in groups of 4 (quatrains)
- Punchlines should land on the last beat of a bar
- Multisyllabic rhymes elevate quality
- Ad-libs add personality and energy

## R&B / Soul

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 65-95 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Love, desire, intimacy, heartbreak, devotion, sensuality, empowerment
**Vocabulary:** Smooth, evocative, sensual. Metaphors for physical and emotional intimacy. Poetic.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. Smooth flow prioritized over strict rhyme. ABCB common.
**Vocal Style:** Melismatic (many notes per syllable), runs, falsetto, breathy, soulful. Emotional range essential.
**Instrument Palette:** Rhodes/electric piano, bass (often synth bass), smooth drums, strings, horns, guitar (clean/funky).
**Suno Style Tags:** `R&B, soulful, smooth, sensual, groovy, neo-soul, contemporary R&B`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Neo-Soul:** Organic, jazzy, live instruments — `neo-soul, organic, jazzy, live instruments, warm`
- **Contemporary R&B:** Modern production, electronic elements — `modern R&B, electronic, atmospheric, moody`
- **Classic Soul:** Motown-influenced, horns, warmth — `classic soul, Motown, warm, horn section, retro`
- **PBR&B / Alternative R&B:** Experimental, atmospheric — `alternative R&B, experimental, ethereal, dark`

## Country

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 80-140 BPM (ballads on low end, up-tempo on high end)
**Lyrical Themes:** Home, family, love/heartbreak, trucks/roads, small towns, drinking, faith, nostalgia, working class life
**Vocabulary:** Conversational, Southern dialect, colloquial. Concrete imagery (dirt roads, pickup trucks, front porches, whiskey). Storytelling focus.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate to high. ABAB and ABCB standard. Clean, satisfying rhymes preferred.
**Vocal Style:** Twangy, warm, often with a Southern accent. Male and female. Storytelling delivery. Emotional sincerity.
**Instrument Palette:** Acoustic guitar, electric guitar (Telecaster), fiddle/violin, pedal steel guitar, banjo, mandolin, bass, drums, dobro, harmonica.
**Suno Style Tags:** `country, acoustic guitar, twangy, heartfelt, pedal steel, fiddle, Nashville`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Bro-Country:** Party-focused, pop-influenced — `bro-country, upbeat, party, modern country`
- **Outlaw Country:** Gritty, rebellious, raw — `outlaw country, gritty, raw, rebellious, 70s`
- **Bluegrass:** Acoustic, fast picking, harmonies — `bluegrass, acoustic, banjo, mandolin, fast-picking`
- **Country Pop:** Polished, crossover — `country pop, polished, catchy, radio-friendly`
- **Americana:** Roots-based, literary — `Americana, roots, folk-country, storytelling`
- **Red Dirt / Texas Country:** Independent, Southern rock influenced — `red dirt country, Texas, Southern rock, independent`

**Key Tips:**
- Specificity is KING in country — name the town, the truck, the bar
- Storytelling songs are highly valued
- The bridge often delivers the twist or emotional revelation
- Conversational tone — write like you're telling a friend a story
- Imagery of the American landscape

## EDM / Electronic

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Buildup → Drop → Breakdown → Buildup → Drop → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 120-150 BPM (varies wildly by subgenre)
**Lyrical Themes:** Euphoria, night life, love, escape, unity, transcendence. Often abstract/minimal.
**Vocabulary:** Simple, repetitive, anthemic. Short phrases that work as chants. Abstract/atmospheric.
**Rhyme Density:** Low to moderate. Rhythm and repetition matter more than rhyme complexity.
**Vocal Style:** Often processed (vocoder, auto-tune, chopped). Female vocals common. Breathy, ethereal. Sometimes spoken word.
**Instrument Palette:** Synthesizers, drum machines, bass drops, pads, arpeggios, samples, risers, sweeps.
**Suno Style Tags:** `electronic, EDM, synth, dance, 128 BPM, bass drop, euphoric`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **House:** Four-on-the-floor, groovy, 120-128 BPM — `house, deep house, four-on-the-floor, groovy`
- **Techno:** Repetitive, industrial, dark, 130-140 BPM — `techno, dark, industrial, repetitive, hypnotic`
- **Trance:** Uplifting, melodic, building, 130-140 BPM — `trance, uplifting, melodic, euphoric, arpeggiated`
- **Dubstep:** Heavy bass, wobble, half-time, 140 BPM — `dubstep, heavy bass, wobble, aggressive, half-time`
- **Drum & Bass:** Fast breakbeats, 170-180 BPM — `drum and bass, fast, breakbeats, rolling bass, 174 BPM`
- **Future Bass:** Lush chords, pitched vocals, 140-160 BPM — `future bass, lush, pitched vocals, bright, energetic`
- **Lo-fi / Chillhop:** Relaxed, jazzy, 75-90 BPM — `lo-fi, chillhop, relaxed, jazzy, tape hiss, vinyl crackle`
- **Synthwave / Retrowave:** 80s-inspired, nostalgic, 100-120 BPM — `synthwave, retrowave, 80s, neon, analog synth, nostalgic`

**Key Tips:**
- Lyrics are secondary to the beat and drop
- Vocal hooks should be short and chantable
- Repetition is structural, not lazy
- Building tension before the drop is crucial
- "Less is more" with lyrics in EDM

## Folk

**Typical Structure:** Verse → Verse → Verse → Verse (strophic) or Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus
**Tempo Range:** 80-120 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Nature, journey, love, social issues, history, community, mythology, labor, protest, personal reflection
**Vocabulary:** Poetic, literary, narrative. Archaic words sometimes used. Earthy, grounded.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. ABCB (ballad meter) very common. Clean, natural rhymes.
**Vocal Style:** Natural, unprocessed, warm. Harmonies (often close harmony). Storytelling delivery. Authentic.
**Instrument Palette:** Acoustic guitar, banjo, fiddle, mandolin, upright bass, harmonica, dulcimer, tin whistle, accordion, hand percussion.
**Suno Style Tags:** `folk, acoustic, warm, storytelling, fingerpicked guitar, harmonies, earthy`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Indie Folk:** Modern, atmospheric, layered — `indie folk, atmospheric, modern, layered, ethereal`
- **Traditional Folk:** Old-world, historical — `traditional folk, Celtic, Appalachian, old-time`
- **Folk Rock:** Electric elements, driving — `folk rock, electric, driving, Dylan-esque`
- **Anti-Folk:** Quirky, lo-fi, subversive — `anti-folk, lo-fi, quirky, irreverent`
- **Contemporary Folk:** Singer-songwriter, confessional — `contemporary folk, singer-songwriter, intimate, confessional`

## Blues

**Typical Structure:** 12-bar blues (AAB) or Verse → Verse → Verse (strophic)
**Tempo Range:** 60-130 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Heartbreak, suffering, loneliness, hard times, travel, desire, resilience, the devil, crossroads
**Vocabulary:** Colloquial, Southern dialect, double entendres, vivid imagery. Pain expressed through everyday language.
**Rhyme Density:** High within the AAB structure (first two lines nearly identical, third line provides the payoff).
**Vocal Style:** Raw, emotive, gritty. Bent notes, moaning, call-and-response. Deep feeling over technical perfection.
**Instrument Palette:** Electric/acoustic guitar (bends, slides), harmonica, piano/organ, bass, drums (shuffle rhythm), horn section.
**Suno Style Tags:** `blues, raw, gritty, electric guitar, shuffle rhythm, soulful, delta blues`

### Classic 12-Bar Blues Lyric Pattern (AAB)

```
[Verse 1]
Woke up this morning, found my baby gone (A)
I said I woke up this morning, found my baby gone (A)
Now I'm sitting here wondering what I did so wrong (B)
```

The first line states the situation. The second line repeats it (building tension). The third line delivers the payoff.

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Delta Blues:** Acoustic, raw, solo — `delta blues, acoustic guitar, raw, solo, Mississippi`
- **Chicago Blues:** Electric, band, urban — `Chicago blues, electric, urban, horn section, gritty`
- **Blues Rock:** Louder, rock-influenced — `blues rock, heavy, electric guitar, power trio`
- **Jazz Blues:** Sophisticated harmony — `jazz blues, sophisticated, horn section, walking bass`

## Metal

**Typical Structure:** Intro (riff) → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Solo → Bridge → Breakdown → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 100-220+ BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Darkness, death, power, mythology, war, inner demons, social rage, fantasy, existentialism
**Vocabulary:** Intense, dramatic, literary. Mythological and dark imagery. Can be cerebral or visceral.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. Rhythmic power of the words matters more than rhyme scheme. Internal rhymes common.
**Vocal Style:** Screaming, growling, clean vocals, falsetto (power metal). Range from melodic to extreme.
**Instrument Palette:** Distorted/down-tuned guitars, double-kick drums, bass guitar, orchestral elements (symphonic metal).
**Suno Style Tags:** `metal, heavy, distorted guitar, double bass drums, aggressive, powerful`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Heavy Metal:** Classic, melodic, powerful — `heavy metal, powerful, melodic, 80s metal`
- **Thrash Metal:** Fast, aggressive, political — `thrash metal, fast, aggressive, shredding, 200 BPM`
- **Death Metal:** Extreme, guttural, blast beats — `death metal, guttural vocals, blast beats, brutal`
- **Black Metal:** Atmospheric, shrieked, tremolo — `black metal, atmospheric, shrieked vocals, tremolo picking`
- **Power Metal:** Epic, fantasy, soaring vocals — `power metal, epic, soaring vocals, fantasy, triumphant`
- **Doom Metal:** Slow, heavy, crushing — `doom metal, slow, heavy, crushing, 60 BPM`
- **Metalcore:** Breakdowns, screamed/clean mix — `metalcore, breakdowns, screaming, melodic chorus`
- **Nu-Metal:** Rap elements, drop-tuned, angsty — `nu-metal, drop-tuned, rap-rock, aggressive, angsty`
- **Symphonic Metal:** Orchestral, cinematic — `symphonic metal, orchestral, cinematic, epic, choir`
- **Progressive Metal:** Complex, technical — `progressive metal, complex, odd time signatures, technical`

## Jazz

**Typical Structure:** Head (melody) → Solos (over chord changes) → Head (reprise) — OR — AABA form
**Tempo Range:** 60-200+ BPM (ballads to bebop)
**Lyrical Themes:** Love, night, city life, sophistication, longing, cleverness, double meanings
**Vocabulary:** Sophisticated, witty, urbane. Clever wordplay. Internal rhymes. Poetic.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate to high. Clever rhymes valued. Unexpected pairings.
**Vocal Style:** Smooth, scat singing, improvised phrasing, behind-the-beat delivery. Swing feel.
**Instrument Palette:** Piano/Rhodes, upright bass, drums (brushes/ride), saxophone, trumpet, trombone, vibraphone, guitar (clean).
**Suno Style Tags:** `jazz, smooth, sophisticated, piano trio, walking bass, brush drums, swing`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Smooth Jazz:** Accessible, mellow — `smooth jazz, mellow, saxophone, late-night`
- **Bebop:** Fast, complex, virtuosic — `bebop, fast, complex, virtuosic, 200 BPM`
- **Cool Jazz:** Laid-back, West Coast — `cool jazz, laid-back, mellow, West Coast`
- **Bossa Nova:** Brazilian-influenced, gentle — `bossa nova, Brazilian, gentle, nylon guitar, 130 BPM`
- **Jazz Fusion:** Rock/funk elements — `jazz fusion, electric, funky, experimental`
- **Vocal Jazz:** Standards, torch songs — `vocal jazz, standards, intimate, crooned, classic`

## Reggae

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 80-110 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Peace, love, resistance, social justice, Rastafari spirituality, unity, nature, oppression
**Vocabulary:** Jamaican Patois elements, spiritual language, political consciousness, natural imagery
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. Natural flow. ABCB common. Repetitive choruses.
**Vocal Style:** Relaxed, melodic, warm. Toasting (rhythmic chanting). Group harmonies. Call and response.
**Instrument Palette:** Rhythm guitar (off-beat "skank"), bass (prominent, melodic), drums (one-drop rhythm), organ, horns, percussion.
**Suno Style Tags:** `reggae, offbeat guitar, one-drop rhythm, warm, island, bass-heavy, Jamaican`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Roots Reggae:** Traditional, spiritual, political — `roots reggae, spiritual, conscious, traditional`
- **Dancehall:** Up-tempo, digital, party — `dancehall, upbeat, digital, party, toasting`
- **Dub:** Spacious, echo-heavy, instrumental — `dub, spacious, echo, reverb, bass-heavy, instrumental`
- **Ska:** Fast, horn-driven, 160+ BPM — `ska, fast, horn section, upbeat, 2-tone`
- **Reggaeton:** Latin-influenced, dembow rhythm — `reggaeton, dembow, Latin, 95 BPM, urban`

## Latin

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 85-130 BPM (varies by subgenre)
**Lyrical Themes:** Love, passion, dancing, romance, partying, sensuality, cultural pride
**Vocabulary:** Spanish (or Spanglish). Passionate, rhythmic, dance-oriented. Romantic imagery.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate to high. Spanish naturally rhymes easily. Flowing.
**Vocal Style:** Passionate, expressive, rhythmic. Both male and female. Sometimes spoken/rapped sections.
**Instrument Palette:** Congas, bongos, timbales, claves, guitar (nylon/electric), brass, piano, bass, guiro.
**Suno Style Tags:** `Latin, tropical, rhythmic, passionate, congas, brass, Spanish vocals`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Salsa:** Afro-Cuban, horn-heavy, danceable — `salsa, Afro-Cuban, horns, piano montuno, danceable`
- **Bachata:** Romantic, guitar-driven — `bachata, romantic, guitar, Dominican, sensual`
- **Reggaeton:** Urban, dembow beat — `reggaeton, dembow, urban, 95 BPM, perreo`
- **Latin Pop:** Polished, radio-friendly — `Latin pop, polished, catchy, romantic, modern`
- **Cumbia:** Accordion, shuffling rhythm — `cumbia, accordion, shuffling rhythm, festive`
- **Bossa Nova:** Brazilian, gentle, jazz-influenced — `bossa nova, Brazilian, gentle, sophisticated`
- **Mariachi:** Trumpets, violins, Mexican — `mariachi, trumpet, violin, Mexican, passionate`

## K-Pop

**Typical Structure:** Intro → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Rap Bridge → Dance Break → Final Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 100-140 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Love, self-confidence, heartbreak, youth, dreams, empowerment, party, fan relationships
**Vocabulary:** Korean (with English phrases mixed in). Catchy, trendy, aspirational. Fan culture language.
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. Korean rhyming conventions differ from English. English hooks common.
**Vocal Style:** Polished, high-register, powerful. Rap sections. Multiple vocal colors in one song (different members). Ad-libs.
**Instrument Palette:** Heavy electronic production, synths, programmed drums, bass drops, orchestral hits, guitar accents.
**Suno Style Tags:** `K-pop, energetic, polished, catchy, dance, electronic, powerful vocals, rap verse`

**Key Tips:**
- Multiple contrasting sections (genre-switching within one song)
- Rap verses are almost mandatory
- Dance breaks (instrumental) are common
- English-language hooks for international appeal
- Maximalist production aesthetic

## Indie

**Typical Structure:** Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus (or more free-form)
**Tempo Range:** 90-140 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Introspection, mundane beauty, alienation, relationships, irony, nature, existential musing
**Vocabulary:** Literary, conversational, quirky. Unexpected word choices. Avoids cliches. Self-aware.
**Rhyme Density:** Low to moderate. Free verse common. Prioritizes authenticity over rhyme.
**Vocal Style:** Natural, sometimes imperfect, distinctive. Quirky timbres valued. Both male and female.
**Instrument Palette:** Guitars (clean to fuzzy), synths, bass, drums, keyboards, strings, unusual instruments (glockenspiel, banjo, brass).
**Suno Style Tags:** `indie, lo-fi, dreamy, atmospheric, jangly guitar, authentic, bedroom pop`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Indie Pop:** Catchy, bright, accessible — `indie pop, bright, catchy, jangly, upbeat`
- **Indie Rock:** Guitar-driven, energetic — `indie rock, guitar-driven, energetic, raw`
- **Indie Folk:** Acoustic, warm, intimate — `indie folk, acoustic, warm, intimate, fingerpicked`
- **Dream Pop:** Ethereal, reverb-heavy, hazy — `dream pop, ethereal, reverb-heavy, hazy, shoegaze`
- **Bedroom Pop:** Lo-fi, intimate, DIY — `bedroom pop, lo-fi, intimate, DIY, homemade`
- **Shoegaze:** Wall of noise, dreamy, distorted — `shoegaze, wall of noise, dreamy, distorted guitars, reverb`
- **Math Rock:** Complex rhythms, angular — `math rock, complex rhythms, angular, tapping, odd meter`

## Punk

**Typical Structure:** Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → (Bridge optional) → Chorus → Outro
**Tempo Range:** 150-200+ BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Anti-authority, DIY, anger, social critique, alienation, nihilism, humor
**Vocabulary:** Direct, confrontational, profane. Short sentences. Sloganeering. Sarcastic.
**Rhyme Density:** High. Simple, punchy rhymes. AABB couplets common.
**Vocal Style:** Shouted, raw, nasal, sneering. Not technically "good" — authenticity over polish.
**Instrument Palette:** Distorted guitar (power chords), bass, fast drums, minimal production.
**Suno Style Tags:** `punk rock, fast, raw, aggressive, distorted, DIY, 180 BPM`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Pop Punk:** Melodic, catchy, youthful — `pop punk, melodic, catchy, youthful, energetic`
- **Hardcore:** Faster, heavier, screamed — `hardcore punk, fast, heavy, screamed, aggressive`
- **Post-Punk:** Atmospheric, darker, arty — `post-punk, atmospheric, dark, angular, bass-driven`
- **Ska Punk:** Horn-driven, upbeat — `ska punk, horns, upbeat, fast, two-tone`
- **Emo:** Emotional, confessional, melodic — `emo, emotional, confessional, melodic, vulnerable`

## Gospel

**Typical Structure:** Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge (Vamp) → Chorus → Outro (Extended Worship)
**Tempo Range:** 70-140 BPM
**Lyrical Themes:** Faith, praise, worship, grace, salvation, struggle, hope, testimony, God's love
**Vocabulary:** Biblical, spiritual, uplifting. "Lord," "grace," "mercy," "salvation," "glory."
**Rhyme Density:** Moderate. Natural flow. Call and response patterns.
**Vocal Style:** Powerful, belted, melismatic. Choir harmonies. Call and response. Spirited, emotional.
**Instrument Palette:** Piano/organ (Hammond), drums, bass, choir, clapping, tambourine, brass.
**Suno Style Tags:** `gospel, choir, uplifting, powerful vocals, Hammond organ, spiritual, soulful`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Traditional Gospel:** Church choir, organ — `traditional gospel, church choir, organ, reverent`
- **Contemporary Gospel:** Modern production, R&B-influenced — `contemporary gospel, modern, R&B-influenced, worship`
- **Southern Gospel:** Country-influenced, quartet — `Southern gospel, country, quartet, harmonies`
- **Gospel Rap:** Hip-hop with gospel themes — `gospel rap, hip-hop, faith-based, uplifting, urban`

## Classical / Orchestral

**Typical Structure:** Through-composed or multi-movement (classical forms). For songs: Aria structure, Lied form.
**Tempo Range:** Highly variable (Largo 40-60, Adagio 60-80, Andante 80-100, Allegro 120-160, Presto 170+)
**Lyrical Themes:** (If vocal) Love, nature, death, mythology, poetry, existential themes
**Vocabulary:** Elevated, poetic, literary. Often draws from great poetry.
**Rhyme Density:** Varies. Follows the source poem's structure.
**Vocal Style:** Operatic (soprano, alto, tenor, baritone, bass). Trained technique. Vibrato. Projection.
**Instrument Palette:** Full orchestra: strings (violin, viola, cello, bass), woodwinds (flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon), brass (trumpet, horn, trombone, tuba), percussion (timpani, cymbals), harp, piano.
**Suno Style Tags:** `orchestral, cinematic, epic, strings, brass, classical, symphonic, dramatic`

**Subgenre Variations:**
- **Film Score:** Cinematic, emotional, narrative — `film score, cinematic, dramatic, emotional, epic orchestra`
- **Neoclassical:** Modern + classical — `neoclassical, piano, modern classical, ambient, minimal`
- **Opera:** Dramatic, vocal, theatrical — `opera, dramatic, soprano, orchestral, theatrical`
- **Choral:** Choir-focused — `choral, choir, sacred, harmonies, cathedral`
- **Chamber Music:** Intimate, small ensemble — `chamber music, intimate, string quartet, delicate`
