# Step-by-Step Bankruptcy Process and Timeline in California

## Overview

Filing for bankruptcy in California is a multi-step federal court process unfolding over weeks, months, or years depending on the chapter filed. This guide walks through every stage from pre-filing preparation through final discharge.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Filing Preparation

### Step 1 — Assess Your Situation

**Timeline: 1–4 weeks before filing**

Before filing, evaluate:
- Total debt amount, types (secured vs. unsecured), and creditor identities
- Monthly income, expenses, and any disposable income
- Asset inventory (home equity, vehicles, bank accounts, retirement)
- Alternatives: debt negotiation, creditor hardship programs, debt consolidation

**Consult a California bankruptcy attorney** — many offer free initial consultations.

---

### Step 2 — Complete Mandatory Credit Counseling

**Timeline: Must be completed within 180 days before filing**

- Duration: Typically 1–2 hours; online, phone, or in-person
- Cost: Usually $10–$50; free for those who cannot afford it
- Provider: Must be on the U.S. Trustee's approved list for your California district (**www.justice.gov/ust**)
- You receive a certificate of completion that must be filed with the petition

---

### Step 3 — Gather Required Financial Documents

| Document | Time Period |
|----------|------------|
| Pay stubs or earnings statements | Last 60 days |
| Federal and California state tax returns | Last 2–3 years |
| Bank and investment account statements | Last 3–6 months |
| Retirement account statements | Current |
| Mortgage statements and deed of trust | Current |
| Vehicle titles and loan statements | Current |
| All credit card and loan statements | All outstanding |
| Medical bills and collection notices | All outstanding |
| Court judgments or wage garnishment orders | All current/recent |

---

### Step 4 — Complete All Bankruptcy Petition Forms

Complete the Official Bankruptcy Forms (see the Required Forms file). Key forms:
- **Voluntary Petition (Form 101)** — Core petition
- **Schedules A/B through J** — All assets, liabilities, income, expenses
- **Statement of Financial Affairs (Form 107)** — Financial history
- **Means Test Forms (Form 122A-1 / 122A-2)** — Chapter 7 eligibility
- **Chapter 13 Plan (Form 113 or local form)** — If filing Chapter 13

**Accuracy is critical.** Signing the petition is done under penalty of perjury. Omitting assets or falsifying information can result in denial of discharge or criminal prosecution for bankruptcy fraud.

---

## Phase 2: Filing the Petition

### Step 5 — File the Petition with the Court

**Timeline: Day 0**

File with the appropriate California bankruptcy district court (see the California Courts file).

#### Filing Fees
- Chapter 7: **$338**
- Chapter 13: **$313**
- Installment payments: Up to 4 installments (Form 103A)
- Fee waiver: Chapter 7 only, income below 150% of poverty line (Form 103B)

#### Immediate Effect — Automatic Stay
The moment the petition is filed, the **automatic stay** (11 U.S.C. § 362) takes effect. All collection actions, lawsuits, foreclosures, and garnishments must immediately cease.

**Notify creditors, employers, and mortgage servicers right away.**

---

### Step 6 — Case Is Assigned and Notice Is Sent

**Timeline: 1–5 days after filing**

The court:
1. Assigns a **case number**
2. Assigns a **trustee**
3. Sends official notice to all listed creditors
4. Sets the **341 Meeting of Creditors** date (typically 20–40 days after filing)

---

### Step 7 — Submit Tax Returns and Pay Stubs to Trustee

**Timeline: Within 7 days of filing (or before the 341 meeting)**

- Mail or upload copies of your most recent **federal tax return** and **60 days of pay stubs** to the trustee
- Chapter 13 trustees may request additional documentation

---

## Phase 3: The 341 Meeting of Creditors

### Step 8 — Attend the 341 Meeting

**Timeline: 20–40 days after filing**

The 341 Meeting is conducted by the trustee — not before a judge. Typical consumer cases last **5–15 minutes**.

#### What to Bring
- Government-issued **photo ID** (driver's license, passport)
- **Social Security card** or other proof of SSN
- Any documents the trustee has requested

#### What to Expect
The trustee places you under oath and asks:
- Did you review your petition and schedules for accuracy?
- Did you list all assets and debts?
- Have you filed bankruptcy before?
- Are there anticipated inheritances, lawsuits, or insurance proceeds?
- Have you transferred any property in the past 2–4 years?

Creditors may attend and ask questions but rarely do in consumer cases.

**Chapter 13 specific:** The trustee also evaluates your proposed repayment plan at or shortly after the 341 meeting.

---

## Phase 4: Post-341 Period

### Step 9 — Trustee Reviews Assets (Chapter 7)

The Chapter 7 trustee determines whether non-exempt assets exist for liquidation. Most California consumer cases are **"no-asset" cases** — all assets are exempt.

If there are non-exempt assets:
- Trustee liquidates them; creditors get 90 days to file proofs of claim
- Asset cases take longer: 6–18 months

### Step 10 — Plan Confirmation (Chapter 13 Only)

**Timeline: 45–90 days after filing**

The judge confirms the plan if it meets all legal requirements:
- Feasible based on debtor's income
- Pays priority creditors in full
- Pays unsecured creditors at least what they would receive in Chapter 7
- Commits all projected disposable income

Debtor must begin plan payments **within 30 days of filing**, even before confirmation.

### Step 11 — Creditor Objection Deadlines

**Timeline: 60 days after the 341 meeting (Chapter 7)**

Creditors wishing to challenge dischargeability of a specific debt must file an **adversary proceeding** within 60 days of the first date set for the 341 meeting.

---

## Phase 5: Completing the Case

### Step 12 — Complete Debtor Education Course

All individual debtors must complete a **Personal Financial Management Course** from a U.S. Trustee-approved provider before discharge.
- Duration: Approximately 2 hours
- Cost: $10–$50
- Certificate filed with court using **Official Form 423**

---

### Step 13 — Discharge Order Entered

**Chapter 7:** Approximately 60–90 days after the 341 meeting; total ~3–6 months from filing

**Chapter 13:** After successful completion of the 3–5 year plan

The discharge order permanently enjoins creditors from further collection on discharged debts.

---

### Step 14 — Case Closed

The court administratively closes the case. The automatic stay ends. Any liens not dealt with during the case remain enforceable against property.

---

## Complete Timeline Summary

### Chapter 7

| Stage | When |
|-------|------|
| Credit counseling | Up to 180 days before filing |
| File petition | Day 0 |
| Automatic stay | Immediate |
| 341 Meeting | Days 20–40 |
| Creditor objection deadline | 60 days after 341 meeting |
| Debtor education complete | Before discharge |
| Discharge order | ~60–90 days after 341 meeting |
| **Total: filing to discharge** | **~3–6 months** |

### Chapter 13

| Stage | When |
|-------|------|
| Credit counseling | Up to 180 days before filing |
| File petition | Day 0 |
| First plan payment due | Within 30 days of filing |
| 341 Meeting | Days 20–45 |
| Plan confirmation hearing | Days 45–90 |
| Ongoing plan payments | Monthly for 3–5 years |
| Debtor education | Before discharge |
| Discharge order | After plan completion |
| **Total: filing to discharge** | **~3–5 years** |

## After Discharge: Rebuilding Credit

1. **Monitor credit reports** — Free reports at AnnualCreditReport.com; dispute inaccurate entries
2. **Open a secured credit card** — Deposit-backed cards rebuild credit history
3. **Maintain on-time payments** — Payment history is the most important credit factor
4. **Avoid high-interest predatory lenders** — Many target recent bankruptcy filers
5. **Consider a credit union** — More likely to offer fair terms to post-bankruptcy borrowers

**Chapter 7 stays on credit report for 10 years; Chapter 13 for 7 years** from the filing date.
