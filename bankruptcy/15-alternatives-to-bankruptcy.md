# Alternatives to Bankruptcy

## Overview

Bankruptcy is a powerful tool, but it's not always the best option. Depending on the type and amount of debt, income, assets, and personal circumstances, several alternatives may provide debt relief without the long-term credit impact of a bankruptcy filing. This guide covers the major alternatives with their pros, cons, and California-specific considerations.

---

## 1. Debt Negotiation / Settlement

### How It Works

- Contact creditors directly (or through a settlement company) and negotiate a lump-sum payment for less than the full balance
- Typical settlements: **25-60% of the balance** for credit card debt
- Most effective when accounts are already delinquent (90-180 days past due)
- Creditors settle because they prefer partial payment over the risk of getting nothing in bankruptcy

### DIY Negotiation Strategy

1. **Stop paying the debt** (if you haven't already) — creditors are more motivated when the account is delinquent
2. **Save cash** — you'll need a lump sum for the settlement offer
3. **Wait 90-180 days** — creditors (or collection agencies who bought the debt) become more flexible
4. **Make your offer** — start at 25-30%, expect to settle at 40-50%
5. **Get the agreement in writing** before sending any money
6. **Pay by cashier's check or money order** — never give direct bank account access
7. **Confirm the account is reported as "settled" or "paid in full"** on your credit report

### California Statute of Limitations on Debt

After the statute of limitations expires, a creditor cannot sue to collect (but can still attempt voluntary collection):

| Debt type | California SOL | Code section |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Written contracts | **4 years** | CCP § 337 |
| Oral contracts | **2 years** | CCP § 339 |
| Credit card debt | **4 years** | CCP § 337 |
| Medical debt | **4 years** (written) | CCP § 337 |
| Promissory notes | **4 years** | Com. Code § 3118 |
| Judgments | **10 years** (renewable) | CCP § 683.020 |
| State tax debt | **20 years** | R&T Code § 19255 |

**Important:**
- SOL starts from the date of last payment or last account activity
- Making a partial payment can RESTART the clock
- Acknowledging the debt in writing can restart the clock
- California SOL applies if the debtor resides in California (CCP § 361)

### Pros and Cons of Debt Settlement

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Pay less than full balance | Damages credit (delinquent accounts) |
| Avoid bankruptcy on record | Tax liability on forgiven amount (1099-C) |
| Faster resolution (months, not years) | Creditors may sue during negotiation |
| Can negotiate yourself (free) | Settlement companies charge 15-25% fees |
| No court involvement | Not all creditors will negotiate |

### Tax Implications

Forgiven debt over $600 generates a **1099-C** (Cancellation of Debt Income). The forgiven amount is taxable unless you qualify for an exclusion:

| Exclusion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Insolvency | Your total debts exceeded total assets at the time of forgiveness |
| Bankruptcy | Debt discharged in Title 11 case |
| Qualified principal residence | Mortgage debt on primary home (limited availability) |
| Qualified farm debt | Farm-related debt |

**File IRS Form 982** to claim the exclusion. The insolvency exclusion is the most commonly used outside of bankruptcy.

## 2. Debt Management Plans (DMPs)

### How They Work

Administered through nonprofit credit counseling agencies:
1. Agency negotiates with creditors for reduced interest rates (often 0-8%) and waived fees
2. Debtor makes one monthly payment to the agency
3. Agency distributes to all enrolled creditors
4. Duration: **3-5 years** to pay off enrolled debt

### What DMPs Can and Cannot Do

| Can do | Cannot do |
|--------|-----------|
| Reduce interest rates | Reduce principal balance |
| Waive late fees and over-limit fees | Stop lawsuits or garnishments |
| Consolidate payments into one | Include secured debt (mortgages, auto) |
| Provide budgeting support | Include student loans or tax debt |
| Stop collection calls (usually) | Prevent credit score impact entirely |

### DMP Terms and Conditions

- Must close enrolled credit card accounts (cannot use them during the plan)
- Cannot open new credit accounts during the plan
- One missed payment may void the agreement
- Agency charges $25-75/month administrative fee

### Finding Legitimate Agencies

- **National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC):** nfcc.org
- **Financial Counseling Association of America (FCAA)**
- California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI) registers debt management providers
- Avoid agencies that charge large upfront fees or guarantee results

## 3. Debt Consolidation Loans

### How They Work

Take out a single loan to pay off multiple debts, leaving one monthly payment at (ideally) a lower interest rate.

### Sources of Consolidation Loans

| Source | Typical rate | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Personal loan (bank/credit union) | 6-20% | Fixed rate, fixed term | Requires decent credit |
| Balance transfer credit card | 0% intro (12-21 months) | No interest during promo | High rate after promo ends; transfer fee (3-5%) |
| Home equity loan/HELOC | 4-9% | Lowest rates; tax-deductible interest | Puts your home at risk |
| 401(k) loan | Prime + 1% | No credit check; pay interest to yourself | Risk to retirement; taxed + 10% penalty if you leave job |
| Peer-to-peer lending | 6-25% | Available for fair credit | Origination fees; variable terms |

### When Consolidation Works

- Total unsecured debt is less than 40% of annual income
- You can qualify for a rate lower than your current average rate
- You have the discipline to not run up new debt on cleared accounts
- You have stable income to make the monthly payment

### When Consolidation Fails

- Using a HELOC to consolidate unsecured debt (converts dischargeable debt to secured)
- Running up credit cards again after consolidating
- Extending the repayment term so much that total interest paid increases
- Paying origination fees that negate the interest savings

## 4. Forbearance and Hardship Programs

### Creditor Hardship Programs

Most major creditors offer hardship programs for temporary financial difficulty:

| Creditor type | Typical options |
|---------------|----------------|
| Credit cards | Reduced APR (often 0-5%), reduced minimum, fee waivers for 6-12 months |
| Mortgage servicers | Forbearance (pause payments 3-12 months), loan modification |
| Auto lenders | Payment deferral (1-3 months added to end of loan) |
| Student loans (federal) | Income-driven repayment, deferment, forbearance |
| Medical providers | Payment plans (often interest-free), financial assistance/charity care |
| Utilities | Payment plans, LIHEAP assistance, shutoff protections |

### California-Specific Protections

- **California Homeowner Bill of Rights:** Requires mortgage servicers to provide a single point of contact, respond to loan modification applications within 30 days, and prohibits dual-tracking (pursuing foreclosure while reviewing modification)
- **Medical debt:** California AB 1020 limits interest on medical debt and requires hospitals to screen patients for financial assistance eligibility
- **Utility shutoff protections:** CPUC regulations limit when utilities can disconnect service; medical baseline programs available

### Mortgage Modifications (Loss Mitigation)

Types of modifications available:

| Modification | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| Rate reduction | Lower interest rate (may be temporary or permanent) |
| Term extension | Extend loan from 30 to 40 years |
| Principal forbearance | Defer portion of principal to end of loan (balloon) |
| Principal reduction | Reduce the principal balance (rare) |
| Capitalization | Add arrears to principal balance |

**Process:**
1. Contact servicer's loss mitigation department
2. Submit complete application (income docs, hardship letter, bank statements)
3. Trial payment plan (3 months of reduced payments)
4. Permanent modification if trial is successful

## 5. California Exemption-Based Strategy (Judgment-Proof)

### What "Judgment-Proof" Means

If a debtor has no non-exempt assets and only exempt income, creditors cannot practically collect even if they obtain a judgment. In this situation, filing bankruptcy may be unnecessary.

### California Exempt Income (Cannot Be Garnished)

| Income type | Exemption |
|-------------|-----------|
| Social Security | 100% exempt (federal) |
| SSI | 100% exempt |
| CalWORKs / TANF | 100% exempt |
| Unemployment benefits | 100% exempt |
| Workers' compensation | 100% exempt |
| Disability benefits (SSDI) | 100% exempt |
| Pension / retirement income | Generally 100% exempt |
| Wages | 75% of disposable earnings exempt (25% can be garnished) |

### When Being Judgment-Proof Is Enough

- You're retired and living on Social Security/pension
- You have no real property or significant personal property
- You don't expect your financial situation to improve significantly
- You can tolerate collection calls (which you can stop with a cease-and-desist letter)

### Limitations

- Judgments last 10 years in California and are renewable — creditors can wait
- If your situation improves, creditors can garnish future wages
- Judgment liens can attach to real property you acquire later
- Does not stop lawsuits from being filed

## 6. Informal Workout / Assignment for Benefit of Creditors

### Informal Workout

Direct negotiations with multiple creditors simultaneously:
- Often facilitated by an attorney
- Creditors agree to reduced payments, extended terms, or partial forgiveness
- No court involvement
- Works best with a small number of cooperative creditors

### Assignment for Benefit of Creditors (ABC)

A state-law alternative to bankruptcy (primarily for businesses):
- Debtor assigns all assets to an assignee (similar to a trustee)
- Assignee liquidates assets and distributes to creditors
- Faster and less expensive than Chapter 7
- No automatic stay, no discharge
- California CCP §§ 493.010-493.060

## 7. State-Specific Collection Protections

### California Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (Rosenthal Act)

**Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1788-1788.33**

California's version of the federal FDCPA, but applies to **original creditors** as well as third-party collectors:

| Protection | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| No harassment | No threats, profanity, or repeated calls to annoy |
| No false representations | Cannot misrepresent amount owed or legal status |
| Validation of debt | Must provide written verification within 30 days of request |
| Cease communication | Must stop contact if debtor sends written cease-and-desist |
| Time restrictions | No calls before 8 AM or after 9 PM |
| Workplace restrictions | Cannot contact debtor at work if employer disapproves |

### California Wage Garnishment Limits

| Debt type | Maximum garnishment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Consumer debt | Lesser of 25% of disposable earnings OR amount exceeding 40× state minimum hourly wage |
| Child/spousal support | Up to 50-65% of disposable earnings |
| Tax debt (IRS) | Based on filing status and dependents |
| Student loans (federal) | 15% of disposable earnings |

### California Bank Levy Exemptions

- $1,788.42 (as of 2024) is automatically exempt from bank levy (CCP § 704.220)
- Public benefits deposited in the account are fully exempt
- Must claim exemption within 15 days of levy notice

| Factor | Settlement | DMP | Consolidation | Ch. 7 | Ch. 13 |
|--------|-----------|-----|---------------|-------|--------|
| **Debt reduction** | 40-75% | Interest only | None | ~100% | Varies |
| **Monthly payment** | Lump sum | Reduced | Single payment | None after discharge | Plan payment |
| **Duration** | 1-3 years | 3-5 years | 2-5 years | 3-6 months | 3-5 years |

## Decision Framework

### File Bankruptcy If:

- Debt exceeds 50% of annual income
- You're being sued or garnished and need immediate relief
- Facing foreclosure and want to save your home (Chapter 13)
- Primarily unsecured debt (credit cards, medical) with few assets
- You've been insolvent for an extended period with no recovery in sight

### Use an Alternative If:

- Debt is manageable (under 30-40% of annual income)
- You have one or two creditors who will negotiate
- Your financial difficulty is temporary (job loss, medical event)
- You have non-exempt assets you want to protect without the complexity of Chapter 13
- Your credit is important for near-term goals (mortgage within 2 years)
- Most of your debt is non-dischargeable anyway (taxes, student loans, support)

### Consult a Bankruptcy Attorney Either Way

- Most bankruptcy attorneys offer free initial consultations
- They can evaluate all options, not just bankruptcy
- California State Bar lawyer referral service: 1-866-442-2529
- Legal Aid Society of your county for low-income individuals
- California Courts Self-Help Center: selfhelp.courts.ca.gov
