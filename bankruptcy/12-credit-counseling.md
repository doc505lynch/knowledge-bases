# Credit Counseling and Debtor Education Requirements

## Overview

The Bankruptcy Abuse Prevention and Consumer Protection Act of 2005 (BAPCPA) requires individuals filing bankruptcy to complete two mandatory educational courses:

1. **Pre-filing credit counseling** — before the bankruptcy petition is filed
2. **Post-filing debtor education (financial management)** — before discharge is entered

Failure to complete either course prevents the bankruptcy from proceeding or results in denial of discharge.

---

## Pre-Filing Credit Counseling

### Legal Requirement

**11 U.S.C. § 109(h)**

Every individual debtor must receive an individual or group briefing from an approved nonprofit credit counseling agency within **180 days before filing** the bankruptcy petition.

### What the Briefing Covers

- Overview of available credit counseling services
- Analysis of the debtor's current financial situation
- Discussion of alternatives to bankruptcy:
  - Debt management plans (DMPs)
  - Debt consolidation
  - Negotiation with creditors
  - Budget adjustments
- Development of a budget (if appropriate)

### Format and Duration

| Format | Availability | Duration |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| In-person | Limited locations | 60-90 minutes |
| Telephone | Widely available | 60-90 minutes |
| Internet/online | Most popular | 30-60 minutes |

### Approved Agencies

Agencies must be approved by the U.S. Trustee Program (USTP) for the district where the case is filed.

**Finding approved agencies for California:**
- U.S. Trustee website: https://www.justice.gov/ust/list-credit-counseling-agencies-approved-pursuant-11-usc-111
- Search by judicial district (California has 4 districts: Northern, Eastern, Central, Southern)

### Cost

- Typical fee: **$10-50** for online courses
- Agencies must provide services regardless of ability to pay
- Fee waivers available for debtors who cannot afford the fee
- Many agencies offer reduced fees or free counseling

### Certificate

Upon completion, the agency issues a **certificate of credit counseling** that must be filed with the bankruptcy court. The certificate is valid for **180 days** from issuance.

**Filing requirement:** The certificate (or a statement that the debtor is exempt) must be filed with the petition or within 14 days after filing. If not filed, the case may be dismissed.

### Exemptions from Pre-Filing Counseling

The court may waive the requirement if:

1. **Exigent circumstances** — debtor faces foreclosure, eviction, wage garnishment, utility shutoff, or similar emergency AND was unable to obtain counseling within 7 days of requesting it
   - Must still complete counseling within 30 days after filing
   - Requires certification describing the exigent circumstances

2. **Incapacity** — debtor is mentally ill or mentally deficient (as defined by the court) to the extent that meaningful participation is impossible
   - Requires certification from an appropriate professional

3. **Disability** — debtor is physically disabled to the extent that in-person counseling is impossible AND no telephone or internet counseling is reasonably available
   - Extremely rare given online availability

4. **Active military duty** in a combat zone

### Joint Cases

If spouses file jointly, **both** debtors must complete credit counseling individually. Some agencies offer joint sessions.

## Post-Filing Debtor Education (Financial Management Course)

### Legal Requirement

**11 U.S.C. § 727(a)(11) (Chapter 7) and § 1328(g) (Chapter 13)**

Before a discharge is entered, every individual debtor must complete an instructional course on personal financial management from an approved provider.

### What the Course Covers

- Budget development and household management
- Money management and use of credit
- How to evaluate financial decisions
- Insurance and risk management
- Saving and investing basics
- Consumer protection and resources

### Timing

| Chapter | When to complete | Deadline |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Chapter 7 | After filing, before discharge | Within ~60 days of filing (discharge typically entered ~90 days after 341 meeting) |
| Chapter 13 | After filing, before final discharge | Before completing the 3-5 year plan (but recommended to do early) |

### Format and Duration

| Format | Duration |
|--------|----------|
| In-person | 2-3 hours |
| Telephone | 2-3 hours |
| Online | 1.5-2.5 hours |

### Approved Providers

Must be approved by the USTP for the applicable district. The provider for debtor education does NOT need to be the same as the pre-filing counseling agency.

### Cost

- Typical fee: **$10-50** for online courses
- Fee waivers available for inability to pay

### Certificate

The provider issues an **Official Form 423 — Certification About a Financial Management Course**. This must be filed with the bankruptcy court.

**If not filed:**
- **Chapter 7:** The court will not enter the discharge. After 45 days, the court typically closes the case without discharge. The debtor may need to reopen the case (additional fees) to file the certificate.
- **Chapter 13:** Discharge will not be entered at plan completion. Must file before final discharge.

## Debt Management Plans (DMPs)

During pre-filing credit counseling, the agency may recommend a DMP as an alternative to bankruptcy.

### How DMPs Work

1. Agency negotiates with creditors for reduced interest rates and waived fees
2. Debtor makes one monthly payment to the agency
3. Agency distributes payments to creditors
4. Typical duration: 3-5 years

### DMP vs. Bankruptcy

| Factor | DMP | Chapter 7 | Chapter 13 |
|--------|-----|-----------|------------|
| Duration | 3-5 years | 3-6 months | 3-5 years |
| Credit impact | Moderate (notation, no bankruptcy) | Severe (10 years on report) | Severe (7 years on report) |
| Debt reduction | Interest reduced; principal paid in full | Most unsecured debt eliminated | Pay portion of unsecured |
| Asset risk | None | Non-exempt assets may be liquidated | Keep all assets |
| Creditor participation | Voluntary | Mandatory | Mandatory |
| Cost | Monthly payment + small fees | Court fees + attorney | Court fees + attorney + plan payments |

### When a DMP Is Better Than Bankruptcy

- Debt is manageable with reduced interest
- Want to avoid bankruptcy on credit report
- Have mostly credit card debt (DMPs work best for this)
- Enough income to maintain payments

### When Bankruptcy Is Better Than a DMP

- Debt is overwhelming (>40% of annual income)
- Facing lawsuits, garnishments, or foreclosure
- Mix of debt types (medical, personal loans, credit cards)
- Need immediate relief (automatic stay)

## Practical Tips

### Before Filing

1. **Complete credit counseling early** — don't wait until the last minute before filing
2. **Keep your certificate** — file it immediately with your petition
3. **Take the counseling seriously** — a DMP may genuinely be a better option
4. **Shop around** — prices vary between approved agencies
5. **Joint filers** — schedule both sessions; many agencies accommodate couples

### After Filing

1. **Complete debtor education promptly** — don't wait until the deadline
2. **File Official Form 423** immediately after completing the course
3. **Keep records** — save the completion certificate and any confirmation emails
4. **Different provider is OK** — you don't need to use the same agency

### Red Flags (Avoid These Agencies)

- Agencies not on the USTP approved list
- Agencies that guarantee debt elimination or specific outcomes
- High fees (>$100) without explanation
- Agencies that pressure you into their own financial products
- Agencies that won't provide a fee waiver when requested
