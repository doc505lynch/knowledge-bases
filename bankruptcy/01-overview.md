# California Bankruptcy Law: Overview

## What Is Bankruptcy?

Bankruptcy is a federal legal process that allows individuals and businesses overwhelmed by debt to either eliminate or restructure what they owe. Although bankruptcy is governed by federal law (Title 11 of the U.S. Code), California has its own specific exemption systems, local court rules, and procedures that significantly affect how cases proceed. Cases are filed in one of California's four federal bankruptcy court districts.

## The Three Main Chapters for Individuals and Businesses

### Chapter 7 — Liquidation Bankruptcy

Chapter 7 is the most common form of personal bankruptcy. A court-appointed trustee reviews your assets, liquidates any non-exempt property, and distributes proceeds to creditors. In exchange, most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) are discharged — meaning legally eliminated. The process typically takes **3 to 6 months** from filing to discharge.

**Best for:** People with little or no disposable income and primarily unsecured debt.

### Chapter 13 — Reorganization / Wage Earner's Plan

Chapter 13 allows individuals with regular income to keep their property and repay all or a portion of their debts through a **3- to 5-year court-approved repayment plan**. You keep your assets but must commit future disposable income to the plan. Upon completion, remaining eligible unsecured debts are discharged.

**Best for:** People with regular income who want to save a home from foreclosure, have non-exempt assets worth protecting, or have debts that cannot be discharged in Chapter 7.

### Chapter 11 — Business Reorganization

Chapter 11 is primarily used by businesses to restructure debts while continuing operations. Individuals with debts exceeding Chapter 13 limits may also use Chapter 11. It is significantly more complex and expensive than Chapters 7 or 13.

**Best for:** Businesses or high-debt individuals needing to restructure rather than liquidate.

### Chapter 12 — Family Farmers and Fishermen

Chapter 12 is a specialized chapter designed for family farmers and commercial fishermen with regular annual income. It functions similarly to Chapter 13 but with higher debt limits and more flexible terms tailored to agricultural income cycles.

## Who Can File in California?

| Chapter | Eligibility Requirements |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Chapter 7 | Must pass the Means Test; no Chapter 7 discharge in prior 8 years |
| Chapter 13 | Regular income; unsecured debt under ~$465,275; secured debt under ~$1,395,875 (amounts adjusted periodically) |
| Chapter 11 | No debt limits for individuals; businesses of any size |
| Chapter 12 | Must qualify as family farmer or fisherman with regular income |

**Note:** Debt limits for Chapter 13 are set by 11 U.S.C. § 109(e) and are periodically adjusted for inflation. Always verify current limits with an attorney or the court's official resources.

## The General Bankruptcy Process in California

1. **Pre-filing credit counseling** — Required within 180 days before filing from an approved agency
2. **Petition preparation** — Complete federal Official Forms disclosing all assets, debts, income, and expenses
3. **Filing with the court** — Submit petition to the appropriate California bankruptcy district; automatic stay immediately takes effect
4. **Trustee assignment** — A trustee is appointed to administer the case
5. **341 Meeting of Creditors** — A short hearing (usually 5–10 minutes) where the trustee and creditors may ask questions under oath
6. **Creditor objection period** — Creditors have 60 days after the 341 meeting to object to discharge (Chapter 7) or confirmation (Chapter 13)
7. **Debtor education course** — Required financial management course before discharge is granted
8. **Discharge** — The court issues the discharge order, eliminating eligible debts

## Key California-Specific Considerations

- **Two exemption systems:** California does not follow the federal exemption scheme. Filers must choose between California's System 1 (CCP § 703) and System 2 (CCP § 704), and cannot mix and match.
- **Four court districts:** California has Northern, Eastern, Central, and Southern bankruptcy districts, each with local rules.
- **Homestead protection:** California dramatically increased its homestead exemption in 2021, now ranging from $300,000 to $600,000 depending on county median home price.
- **Community property state:** California is a community property state, which affects how marital debts and assets are treated.

## Bankruptcy's Effect on Credit

A Chapter 7 bankruptcy remains on your credit report for **10 years** from the filing date. A Chapter 13 remains for **7 years**. While the impact is significant, many filers begin rebuilding credit within 1–2 years post-discharge through secured credit cards and responsible financial management.
