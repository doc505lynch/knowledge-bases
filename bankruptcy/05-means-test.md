# The Bankruptcy Means Test in California

## What Is the Means Test?

The **Means Test** was introduced by BAPCPA 2005 to prevent high-income individuals from filing Chapter 7 when they could reasonably repay debts. It is calculated using **Official Form 122A-1** and, if needed, **Official Form 122A-2**.

Passing the Means Test is required to file Chapter 7. Failing it creates a **presumption of abuse**, which may force dismissal or conversion to Chapter 13.

## Step 1 — Calculate Current Monthly Income (CMI)

CMI is the **average monthly income from all sources** over the **6 calendar months before the filing date**.

### Included in CMI
- Wages, salary, tips, bonuses, overtime
- Net self-employment or business income
- Interest, dividends, royalties
- Rental income (net)
- Pension and retirement income
- Regular contributions from others toward household expenses
- Unemployment compensation

### Excluded from CMI
- **Social Security benefits** (specifically excluded by statute — major advantage for older filers)
- Payments to victims of war crimes or terrorism
- Veterans' disability benefits

## Step 2 — Compare to California Median Income

Annualize CMI (multiply by 12) and compare to California's median income for your household size.

### California Median Income (Approximate — Verify at www.justice.gov/ust)

| Household Size | California Annual Median |
|----------------|------------------------|
| 1 person | ~$75,000 |
| 2 persons | ~$97,000 |
| 3 persons | ~$112,000 |
| 4 persons | ~$130,000 |
| 5 persons | ~$140,000 |
| 6 persons | ~$150,000 |
| Each additional | Add ~$9,000 |

**These figures change frequently — always verify current figures at the U.S. Trustee Program website at time of filing.**

### If Income Is Below the Median — You Pass Automatically
The presumption of abuse does not apply. You may proceed with Chapter 7.

### If Income Exceeds the Median — Proceed to Step 3
You must complete the full Means Test calculation.

## Step 3 — Full Means Test Calculation

Deduct **allowable monthly expenses** from CMI to calculate **monthly disposable income**.

### IRS National and Local Standards

| Expense Category | Standard Type |
|------------------|---------------|
| Food, clothing, personal care | National Standard |
| Housing and utilities | Local Standard (by county) |
| Transportation ownership/lease | Local Standard |
| Transportation operating costs | Local Standard |

California's Local Standards are higher than national averages due to the state's high cost of living.

### Additional Allowed Deductions
- Health insurance, disability insurance, health savings accounts — actual amounts
- Contributions to care of elderly, chronically ill, or disabled household members
- Term life insurance premiums — actual amounts
- Required education expenses for disabled dependents
- Secured debt payments (mortgage, car loans) — contractual amounts
- Priority debt payments (taxes, domestic support)
- Charitable contributions — up to 15% of gross income

### Interpreting Monthly Disposable Income (MDI)

| Monthly Disposable Income | Result |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Less than **$8,175/month** (and less than 25% of nonpriority unsecured debt) | No presumption of abuse — Chapter 7 allowed |
| More than **$13,650/month** | Presumption of abuse — Chapter 7 very difficult |
| Between $8,175–$13,650/month | Additional calculation required |

*(Dollar thresholds adjusted periodically — confirm at time of filing.)*

## What If You Fail the Means Test?

### Option 1 — Rebut with Special Circumstances
Document additional expenses or income adjustments due to:
- Serious medical condition or disability
- Active military duty
- Significant job loss occurring after the measurement period

### Option 2 — Convert to Chapter 13
Convert to a repayment plan. Many attorneys initially file Chapter 7 and convert if necessary.

### Option 3 — Dismiss and Wait
Dismiss the case and refile after circumstances change (income decrease, larger household).

## Exemptions from the Means Test

The Means Test does **not** apply to:
- Disabled veterans whose debts were primarily incurred during active military duty
- National Guard/reserve members on active duty for 90+ days within 540 days before filing
- Debtors whose debts are **primarily business debts** (more than 50% business-related)

## Practical Tips for California Filers

1. **Timing matters:** File in a month after a significant income decrease to lower your 6-month CMI average
2. **Social Security exclusion:** Large Social Security income does not count — major advantage for older filers
3. **Household size matters:** Include all household members (including non-filing spouses and dependents) to increase the median threshold
4. **California Local Standards are high:** The state's high cost of living results in larger housing and transportation deductions
