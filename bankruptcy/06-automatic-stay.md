# The Automatic Stay in California Bankruptcy

## What Is the Automatic Stay?

The **automatic stay** is one of the most immediate and powerful protections bankruptcy provides. The moment a bankruptcy petition is filed, the automatic stay goes into effect automatically by operation of federal law under **11 U.S.C. § 362** — before the judge reviews it, before creditors are notified.

The stay immediately **prohibits virtually all creditors from taking any collection action** against the debtor or the debtor's property.

## What the Automatic Stay Stops

### Creditor Collection Actions
- All telephone calls, letters, emails, and texts attempting to collect debts
- Threats of legal action
- Demands for payment

### Lawsuits and Legal Proceedings
- Filing or continuing civil lawsuits against the debtor in any court
- Enforcing judgments obtained before the bankruptcy filing
- Appeals of pre-petition judgments

### Wage Garnishment
- Stops all wage garnishments immediately — including garnishments already in progress
- Employer must be notified; funds already deducted but not yet sent to the creditor may need to be returned

### Bank Account Levies
- Stops bank levies in progress
- Funds frozen but not yet turned over may be returned to the debtor

### Repossession
- Prevents vehicle repossession from the moment of filing
- If a vehicle was repossessed **before** filing but not yet sold, the debtor may demand its return

### Foreclosure
- Immediately halts foreclosure proceedings, including:
  - Stopping a foreclosure sale that has not yet occurred
  - Preventing the filing of a notice of default
  - Halting the foreclosure auction (even on the courthouse steps, if filed in time)
- **Critical:** Once a foreclosure sale is **completed** and the trustee's deed is delivered, the automatic stay cannot undo it. Timing is everything.

### Utility Shutoffs
- Utility companies (electric, gas, water, telephone) cannot terminate service for the first **20 days** after filing
- After 20 days, the utility may disconnect unless the debtor provides adequate assurance of future payment (typically a deposit)

### Tax Collection
- Stops IRS and California Franchise Tax Board collection actions, including:
  - Tax levies
  - Offset of tax refunds
  - Existing tax liens remain — stay prevents new enforcement action

## What the Automatic Stay Does NOT Stop — Exceptions (§ 362(b))

| Exception | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Criminal proceedings** | Arrest, prosecution, or criminal sentencing |
| **Child support and alimony** | Establishment, modification, and collection of domestic support obligations |
| **Paternity actions** | Legal proceedings to establish paternity |
| **Domestic violence proceedings** | Restraining orders and protective orders |
| **Certain tax proceedings** | Audit and assessment (not collection) of taxes |
| **Pension loans** | Withholding wages to repay an ERISA-qualified pension loan |
| **Evictions with pre-petition judgment** | If landlord obtained judgment for possession before filing, stay may not apply |
| **Securities regulation** | Actions by the SEC and other regulatory bodies |
| **Landlord drug/endangerment** | Eviction based on endangered property or illegal drug use |

## How Long Does the Automatic Stay Last?

### Chapter 7
The stay remains in effect until:
- The case is closed or dismissed, or
- A discharge is granted or denied, or
- A creditor obtains relief from the stay

In a typical Chapter 7 case: approximately **3–4 months**.

### Chapter 13
The stay continues through the **entire repayment plan** (3–5 years) unless lifted by court order.

### Repeat Filers — Reduced Stay Periods

| Prior Dismissals (within 1 year) | Stay Duration |
|----------------------------------|---------------|
| One prior dismissed case | Stay lasts **30 days only** — debtor must get court order to extend |
| Two or more prior dismissed cases | **No automatic stay** — debtor must file a motion to impose the stay |

## Relief from the Automatic Stay

Creditors can ask the court to lift the stay by filing a **Motion for Relief from Automatic Stay**. Grounds include:

1. **"For cause"** — most commonly, lack of equity in the property and property not necessary for reorganization; or failure to make post-petition mortgage payments
2. **Adequate protection** — when collateral is depreciating, court may require the debtor to make adequate protection payments

## Willful Violation — Remedies

If a creditor **knowingly** violates the automatic stay, the debtor may recover:
- **Actual damages** (including attorney fees)
- **Punitive damages** in appropriate cases
- Contempt of court sanctions

## Practical Steps for California Debtors

1. **Notify creditors immediately** — Provide your case number, district, and filing date
2. **Send certified mail** — Document all notifications to establish knowledge of the stay
3. **Contact your employer** — If wages are being garnished, notify payroll immediately with a copy of the filing
4. **Act before a foreclosure sale** — The stay stops a scheduled sale only if filed **before** the sale is completed
5. **Keep records** — If a creditor continues collection after notice, document every contact for a willful violation claim
